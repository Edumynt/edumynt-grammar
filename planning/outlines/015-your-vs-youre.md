## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** "Your" = possessive adjective. "You're" = contraction of "you are."
- **Key rule:** If you can replace with "you are" → you're. Otherwise → your.
- **References:** Swan *Practical English Usage* (entry on your/you're).
- **Common errors:** "Your welcome" → "You're welcome"; "You're book is here" → "Your book is here."
- **Exam relevance:** High.

### 1. Introduction: The Error That Makes Editors Cringe

### 2. The Rule
- **Your** = possessive: your book, your idea, your turn.
- **You're** = you are: you're welcome, you're late, you're the best.

### 3. The Substitution Test
- Replace with "you are":
  - "You're right" = "You are right" → **you're** ✓
  - "Your book" = "You are book" → nonsense → **your** ✓

### 4. Common Mistakes
- "Your welcome" → "You're welcome"
- "Your a good student" → "You're a good student"
- "You're phone is ringing" → "Your phone is ringing"
