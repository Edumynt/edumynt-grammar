## Section Structure

### 1. Introduction: The Most Confused Pair in English
- Even native speakers mix these up. The rule is simpler than you think.

### 2. The Basic Rule
- **Affect** (verb): to influence. "The rain affected the match."
- **Effect** (noun): a result. "The effect of the rain was flooding."

### 3. The Memory Trick
- **A**ffect = **a**ction (verb).
- **E**ffect = r**e**sult (noun).

### 4. The Rare Exceptions
- **Effect** as verb: "to effect change" = to bring about / cause to happen. Formal usage.
- **Affect** as noun: in psychology, "affect" = emotional state. Technical usage.
- For 95% of cases, the verb-noun rule works.

### 5. Common Mistakes
- "The weather had a big affect" → "a big effect"
- "It effected me deeply" → "It affected me deeply"
- "The new policy will effect change" → correct (verb = bring about)
