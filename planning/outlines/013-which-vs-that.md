## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** "That" introduces **restrictive** (essential) clauses — information needed to identify the noun. "Which" introduces **non-restrictive** (extra) clauses — additional info set off by commas.
- **Key rule:** Restrictive (no commas) → that (or which in BrE). Non-restrictive (commas) → which only.
- **References:** Quirk et al. §17.14–17.22; Swan *Practical English Usage* (entry on which/that); *The Chicago Manual of Style* §6.22.
- **Common errors:** "The car which is outside is mine" (restrictive — should be "that" in AmE); "My car, that is outside, is mine" (non-restrictive — should be "which").
- **Exam relevance:** High — tested in error spotting and sentence correction.
- **Overlap warning:** Related to #057 (defining vs non-defining relative clauses) — keep this focused on which/that word choice.

## Section Structure

### 1. Introduction: Two Words for the Same Job — Almost
- "The book that I read" vs "The book, which I read, was great."
- The difference is about necessity and commas.

### 2. Restrictive Clauses — Essential Information
- Identifies WHICH thing you mean: "The car **that** is parked outside is mine."
- Without the clause, you don't know which car.
- No commas. Use **that** (preferred in AmE) or **which** (acceptable in BrE).

### 3. Non-Restrictive Clauses — Extra Information
- Adds bonus info about something already identified: "My car, **which** is parked outside, needs washing."
- The clause can be removed without losing the main point.
- Always uses commas. Always uses **which** (never "that").

### 4. The Comma Test
- If you need commas → which.
- If no commas → that (or which in British English).

### 5. American vs British Usage
- American English: strict — "that" for restrictive, "which" for non-restrictive.
- British English: "which" is often used for both.
- For exams: follow the American distinction (it's what most Indian exams test).

### 6. When Only "That" Works
- After superlatives: "This is the best movie **that** I've seen."
- After "all," "every," "any," "no": "All **that** glitters is not gold."
- After "the only," "the very," "the first": "She was the only person **that** helped."

### 7. Common Mistakes
- "My laptop, that cost ₹50,000, is slow" → "My laptop, **which** cost ₹50,000, is slow"
- "The phone which I bought yesterday is great" → "The phone **that** I bought yesterday is great" (AmE, restrictive)
- Omitting commas in non-restrictive clauses.

### 8. Quick Practice
- 5 sentences — choose which or that, add commas if needed.
- Answer key.

### 9. Summary
- Essential info (no commas) → that. Extra info (commas) → which.
