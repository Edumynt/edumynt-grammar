## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** "Then" = time/sequence. "Than" = comparison.
- **Key rule:** If comparing → than. If about time or what happens next → then.
- **References:** Swan *Practical English Usage* (entry on then/than).
- **Common errors:** "She is taller then him" → "taller than him"; "Than we left" → "Then we left."
- **Exam relevance:** High.
- **Overlap warning:** None — unique topic.

## Section Structure

### 1. Introduction: One Letter, Two Meanings
- "I'd rather stay than go" vs "We ate, then we left."

### 2. "Than" — For Comparatives
- Used with comparative adjectives/adverbs: bigger than, more than, less than, rather than, other than.
- "She is taller than me." "I'd rather walk than drive."

### 3. "Then" — For Time and Sequence
- "We ate, and then we left." "If you're tired, then rest."
- "Then" can also mean "at that time": "Back then, things were different."

### 4. Memory Trick
- Comp**a**rison → **a** → th**a**n.
- Time → **e** → th**e**n.

### 5. Common Mistakes
- "More then" → "more than"
- "Other then" → "other than"
- "Better then" → "better than"
- "Than we went" → "Then we went"
