## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Possessive apostrophes mark ownership or association; placement depends on singular/plural form.
- **Key rule:** Singular: ’s; regular plural ending in s: s’; irregular plural: ’s.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** The girls bag. → The girl’s bag / girls’ bags.; Childrens toys. → Children’s toys.; James book. → James’s book (style may vary).; Its tail (possessive) vs It’s raining (it is).
