## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Mixed conditionals combine one time in the condition with another time in the result.
- **Key rule:** Use mixed forms when cause and result belong to different times.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** If I studied harder, I would have passed. → If I had studied harder, I would have passed.; If I had taken the job, I would be rich now. (past condition, present result); If I were more careful, I wouldn’t have made that mistake. (present trait, past result); Do not mix tenses unless the time frames justify it.
