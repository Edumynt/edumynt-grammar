## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Future continuous shows an action in progress at a future time; future perfect shows completion before a future point.
- **Key rule:** Will be + -ing = in progress; will have + past participle = completed by then.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** By 8 pm, I will be finishing the work. → will have finished.; At 8 pm, I will have watched TV. → will be watching TV.; This time tomorrow, I will travel. → will be travelling.; By next year, she will be completing the course. → will have completed.
