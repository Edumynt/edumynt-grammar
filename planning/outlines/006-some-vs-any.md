## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** "Some" is typically used in affirmative statements; "any" in negatives and questions. But the real distinction is about **assertiveness** and **polarity**.
- **Key rule:** some = affirmative/assertive; any = negative/interrogative/non-assertive.
- **References:** Quirk et al. §5.14–5.17; Swan *Practical English Usage* (entries on some/any); Huddleston & Pullum §5.8.
- **Common errors:** "Do you have some money?" (when expecting "yes" — actually correct); "I have any book" (wrong).
- **Exam relevance:** High — tested in error spotting and fill-in-the-blanks.

## Section Structure

### 1. Introduction: Two Words for an Unspecified Amount
- "I need some water" vs "I don't have any water" — why the switch?

### 2. The Basic Rule
- **Some** in affirmative statements: "I have some books."
- **Any** in negatives: "I don't have any books."
- **Any** in questions: "Do you have any books?"

### 3. The Deeper Logic: Assertive vs Non-Assertive
- "Some" implies the speaker believes something exists / is available.
- "Any" is neutral — it doesn't assume existence.
- This explains the exceptions.

### 4. When "Some" Appears in Questions
- Offers and requests: "Would you like some tea?" (expecting yes).
- When you expect a positive answer: "Did some of you come by car?" (you saw cars).
- Invitations: "Can I have some more?"

### 5. When "Any" Appears in Affirmatives
- "Any" = "it doesn't matter which": "You can take any book." (free choice).
- "Any" after "hardly," "barely," "scarcely" (negative meaning): "I hardly have any time."
- "Any" in conditionals: "If you have any questions, ask."

### 6. "Somebody," "Anyone," "Nothing," etc.
- Same rule applies to compound pronouns: someone/anyone, something/anything, somewhere/anywhere.
- "I saw someone" (affirmative) vs "I didn't see anyone" (negative).

### 7. Common Mistakes
- "I don't have some money" → "I don't have any money"
- "Do you have any tea?" (neutral) vs "Do you have some tea?" (offering) — both correct, different meaning
- "I want any book" → "I want some book" (or "any book" only if "it doesn't matter which")
