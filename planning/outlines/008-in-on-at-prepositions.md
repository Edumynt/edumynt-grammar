## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** "In," "on," and "at" form a scale from **general/broad** to **specific/precise**. This applies to both time and place.
- **Key rule:** in = largest/ broadest (countries, months, years, large areas); on = surface/days/dates; at = specific point (clock times, specific addresses, points).
- **References:** Quirk et al. §6.20–6.35; Swan *Practical English Usage* (entries on in/on/at); Murphy *English Grammar in Use* (Units 113–116).
- **Common errors:** "in Monday," "at June," "in the bus" vs "on the bus."
- **Exam relevance:** Extremely high — prepositions are among the most tested items.
- **Overlap warning:** Related to #007 (since/for) and #043 (error spotting prepositions) — keep focused on in/on/at.

## Section Structure

### 1. Introduction: Three Prepositions, One Logic
- "In India," "on Main Street," "at 42 Main Street" — the logic is the same.
- General → specific.

### 2. Place: The General-to-Specific Scale
- **In:** countries, cities, neighborhoods, rooms, enclosed spaces — in India, in Delhi, in the kitchen, in the box.
- **On:** surfaces, streets, floors, public transport (on a bus/train/plane) — on the table, on Main Street, on the bus, on the 3rd floor.
- **At:** specific points, addresses, buildings as destinations — at 42 Main Street, at the bus stop, at school, at home, at the door.

### 3. Time: The Same Scale
- **In:** months, years, seasons, centuries, parts of day — in June, in 2020, in winter, in the morning.
- **On:** days and dates — on Monday, on 15th August, on my birthday, on Christmas Day.
- **At:** clock times, mealtimes, night, specific moments — at 5 PM, at noon, at night, at lunchtime, at midnight.

### 4. The "At Home" Exception
- "At home" is a fixed expression — not "in home."
- Similarly: at work, at school, at university (as a general activity, not a building).

### 5. Transport: "In" vs "On"
- **On** large public transport: on the bus, on the train, on the plane, on the ship.
- **In** small/private vehicles: in the car, in a taxi, in a helicopter.
- Logic: you can stand/walk on a bus; you're enclosed in a car.

### 6. Common Mistakes
- "in Monday" → "on Monday"
- "at June" → "in June"
- "in the bus" → "on the bus" (public transport)
- "on the car" → "in the car"
- "at the table" (position at) vs "on the table" (surface) — different meanings
- "in night" → "at night"

### 8. Summary
