## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Phrases with as well as add information but do not make the subject plural.
- **Key rule:** The verb agrees with the first/main subject, not the as-well-as phrase.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** Ram as well as Shyam are coming. → Ram as well as Shyam is coming.; The teachers as well as the principal is present. → The teachers ... are present.; The box as well as the books were lost. → The box ... was lost.; My brother, as well as my parents, are invited. → is invited.
