## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** "Advice" (noun, /ədˈvaɪs/) = a recommendation. "Advise" (verb, /ədˈvaɪz/) = to recommend.
- **Key rule:** Same pattern as practice/practise, licence/license, noun ends in -c, verb ends in -s.
- **References:** Swan *Practical English Usage* (entry on advice/advise).
- **Common errors:** "He adviced me" → "He advised me"; "He gave me advise" → "He gave me advice."
- **Exam relevance:** High.
- **Overlap warning:** Related to #020 (practice/practise) — same pattern.

## Section Structure

### 1. Introduction: One Is a Thing, One Is an Action
- "I need advice" (noun) vs "I advise you" (verb).

### 2. The Rule
- **Advice** = noun (thing you give/receive). "She gave me good advice."
- **Advise** = verb (action of recommending). "I advise you to study."

### 3. Pronunciation Difference
- Advice = /s/ sound (like "ice").
- Advise = /z/ sound (like "eyes").
- In spoken English, the pronunciation makes the distinction clear.

### 4. The -C / -S Pattern
- Nouns ending in -c: advice, practice, licence.
- Verbs ending in -s: advise, practise, license.
- (This is the British English pattern; American English uses "practice" for both.)

### 5. Common Mistakes
- "He gave me good advise" → "good advice"
- "I advice you" → "I advise you"
- "She adviced him" → "She advised him"
