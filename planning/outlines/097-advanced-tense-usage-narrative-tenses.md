## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Narrative tenses combine past simple, past continuous, and past perfect to order events and set scenes.
- **Key rule:** Use past continuous for background, past simple for events, past perfect for earlier events.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** I was walking when I saw him.; She had left before I arrived.; The sun was setting, and people were returning home.; I had finished and went out. → I had finished and went out / After I finished...
- **Exam relevance:** Moderate to High
