## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Each focuses on individual members; every focuses on the whole group, but both normally take singular nouns and verbs.
- **Key rule:** Each/every + singular noun + singular verb.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** Each students are ready. → Each student is ready.; Every of them came. → Every one of them came.; Each and every are followed by plural nouns. → Use singular nouns.; The teacher gave every a book. → The teacher gave each student a book.
