## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** A modifier placed too far from the word it describes can create the wrong meaning.
- **Key rule:** Place modifiers next to the word or phrase they modify.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** I only ate two apples. → I ate only two apples.; She saw a dog driving to work. → Driving to work, she saw a dog.; We served sandwiches to children on paper plates. → We served children sandwiches on paper plates.; Almost he solved all questions. → He solved almost all questions.
