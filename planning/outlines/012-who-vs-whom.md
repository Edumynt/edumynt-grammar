## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** "Who" is a subject pronoun (like he/she/they); "whom" is an object pronoun (like him/her/them).
- **Key rule:** If you can replace with "he/she" → who. If "him/her" → whom.
- **References:** Quirk et al. §6.36–6.38; Swan *Practical English Usage* (entry on who/whom); Huddleston & Pullum §5.14.
- **Common errors:** "Whom is coming?" → "Who is coming?"; "Who did you give it to?" (informal) vs "To whom did you give it?" (formal).
- **Exam relevance:** High — tested in error spotting and sentence correction.

## Section Structure

### 1. Introduction: The Pronoun That's Slowly Vanishing
- "Whom" is fading from spoken English — but exams still test it.
- The trick to getting it right is simpler than you think.

### 2. The He/Him Trick
- Replace who/whom with he/him:
  - "Who/Whom is coming?" → "He is coming" → **Who**.
  - "Who/Whom did you call?" → "I called him" → **Whom**.
- If "he" works → who. If "him" works → whom.
- (Same letters: h**e** = wh**o**; h**i**m = wh**o**m.)

### 3. "Who" as Subject
- Performs the action: "Who wrote this?" "The person who called was angry."
- In relative clauses: "The man **who** lives next door" (who = subject of "lives").

### 4. "Whom" as Object
- Receives the action: "Whom did you see?" "The person whom I called."
- After prepositions: "To whom it may concern," "With whom did you go?"
- In relative clauses: "The man **whom** I met" (whom = object of "met").

### 5. When "Whom" Sounds Stiff
- Informal English: "Who did you give it to?" (not "To whom did you give it?").
- Both are grammatically defensible in modern English.
- In formal writing and exams, prefer "whom" for objects.

### 6. The "Who/Whom" Trap in Complex Sentences
- "The student who/whom the teacher praised" → "The teacher praised him" → **whom**.
- "The student who/whom everyone thinks will win" → "Everyone thinks **he** will win" → **who**.
- The trick works even in complex sentences — just isolate the clause.

### 7. Common Mistakes
- "Whom is at the door?" → "Who is at the door?"
- "Who did you send it to?" → "Whom did you send it to?" (formal) / "To whom did you send it?" (very formal)
- "The person whom is responsible" → "The person who is responsible"

### 8. Quick Practice
- 5 sentences — choose who or whom.
- Answer key.

### 9. Summary
- Who = subject (he). Whom = object (him). Use the substitution trick.
