## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Causatives show that someone causes another person to do something or arranges a service.
- **Key rule:** Have/get + object + past participle for services; have/make/get someone do/to do for actions.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** I cut my hair yesterday. → I had my hair cut.; She made me to wait. → made me wait.; I got him repair it. → got him to repair it.; We had the car serviced. (correct)
