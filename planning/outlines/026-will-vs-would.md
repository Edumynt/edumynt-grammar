## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Will marks future, willingness, and prediction; would marks past habit, politeness, hypothetical result, and future-in-the-past.
- **Key rule:** Use will for real future; would for imagined/past/polite meanings.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** If I know, I will tell you. → If I knew, I would tell you.; Would you like tea? (polite) not Will you like tea?; When I was a child, I will play outside. → I would play outside.; He said he will come. → He said he would come.
