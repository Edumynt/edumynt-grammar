## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Past perfect marks the earlier of two past events; past simple narrates the main past sequence.
- **Key rule:** Use had + past participle only when an earlier-past relationship must be clear.
- **References:** Quirk et al., A Comprehensive Grammar of the English Language; Huddleston & Pullum, The Cambridge Grammar of the English Language; Swan, Practical English Usage; Murphy, English Grammar in Use.
- **Common errors:** When I reached, the train left. → had left.; I had met him yesterday. → I met him yesterday.; After he had finished, he left. (correct); She cooked dinner before I had arrived. → before I arrived / had cooked before I arrived.
