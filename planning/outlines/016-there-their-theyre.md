## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** Three homophones: "there" (place/expletive), "their" (possessive), "they're" (contraction of "they are").
- **Key rule:** they're = they are; their = possession; there = place or existential.
- **References:** Swan *Practical English Usage* (entries on there/their/they're).
- **Common errors:** All three are frequently swapped.
- **Exam relevance:** High.

## Section Structure

### 1. Introduction: Three Words, Same Sound, Different Jobs
- A classic trio of confusion.

### 2. "They're" — They Are
- Contraction: "They're coming," "They're happy."
- Test: replace with "they are."

### 3. "Their" — Possession
- Belonging to them: "Their house," "Their idea."
- Test: does it show ownership?

### 4. "There" — Place or Existential
- Place: "Put it there," "Over there."
- Existential (no real meaning): "There is a problem," "There are many options."
- Test: if it's not "they are" and not possession → there.

### 5. Memory Trick
- **They're** has an apostrophe → contraction → "they are."
- **Their** has "heir" inside → inheritance → possession.
- **There** has "here" inside → both are about place.

### 6. Common Mistakes
- "Their coming" → "They're coming"
- "There house" → "Their house"
- "They're is a problem" → "There is a problem"
