## Research / Grounding Notes

- **Core concept:** "Its" (no apostrophe) = possessive pronoun (like his, her, their). "It's" (with apostrophe) = contraction of "it is" or "it has."
- **Key rule:** If you can replace with "it is" or "it has" → it's. Otherwise → its.
- **References:** Swan *Practical English Usage* (entry on its/it's); Quirk et al. §6.14.
- **Common errors:** "The dog wagged it's tail" → "its tail"; "Its a nice day" → "It's a nice day."
- **Exam relevance:** Very high — one of the most common errors tested.
- **Overlap warning:** Related to #068 (possessive apostrophe rules) — keep focused on its/it's.

## Section Structure

### 1. Introduction: The Most Common Apostrophe Error in English
- Even native speakers mix these up. Here's why — and how to fix it.

### 2. Why It's Confusing
- Normally, apostrophe + s = possessive: "John's book," "the cat's tail."
- But "its" breaks this rule — the possessive has NO apostrophe.
- Reason: "it's" was already taken by the contraction of "it is."

### 3. The Simple Test
- Replace with "it is" or "it has":
  - "It's raining" = "It is raining" → **it's** ✓
  - "The dog wagged its tail" = "The dog wagged it is tail" → nonsense → **its** ✓

### 4. "It's" — The Contraction
- "It's" = "it is": "It's cold today."
- "It's" = "it has": "It's been a long day."
- That's ALL "it's" ever means.

### 5. "Its" — The Possessive
- "Its" shows ownership: "The company changed its policy."
- Like "his," "her," "our," "their" — no apostrophe on any of them.

### 6. Common Mistakes
- "The cat licked it's paw" → "its paw"
- "Its been great" → "It's been great"
- "The tree lost it's leaves" → "its leaves"

### 7. Quick Practice
- 5 sentences — choose its or it's.
- Answer key.

### 8. Summary
- It's = it is / it has. Its = possessive. The substitution test never fails.
