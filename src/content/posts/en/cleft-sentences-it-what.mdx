## Introduction: Splitting the Sentence to Highlight What Matters

A cleft sentence splits one idea into two parts to emphasize one element.

> I met Rahul yesterday. → **It was Rahul that** I met yesterday.  
> I need more time. → **What I need is** more time.

The information is the same. The focus shifts. This article covers the two main types: **it-clefts** and **wh-clefts**.

---

## Core Meaning and Rule

> **Rule box:** Use clefts to highlight focused information. **It + be + focus + that/who clause.** **Wh-clause + be + focus.**

| Type | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| It-cleft | It + be + focus + that/who + clause | It was **Rahul that** I met. |
| Wh-cleft | What + subject + verb + be + focus | What I **need is** more time. |

---

## Forms, Patterns, and Sentence Position

### 1. It-Cleft: Emphasizing the Object

> I met **Rahul** yesterday. → It was **Rahul that** I met yesterday.  
> She needs **help**. → It is **help that** she needs.

### 2. It-Cleft: Emphasizing the Subject

> **Rahul** helped me. → It was **Rahul who** helped me.  
> **The noise** woke me up. → It was **the noise that** woke me up.

### 3. It-Cleft: Emphasizing Time

> I met him **yesterday**. → It was **yesterday that** I met him.

Use **that** (not *when*) for time in formal it-clefts.

### 4. Wh-Cleft (Pseudo-Cleft)

> I need **more time**. → What I need **is** more time.  
> She wants **honesty**. → What she wants **is** honesty.  
> He did **leave early**. → What he did **was leave** early.

In wh-clefts, the verb after *be* is a bare infinitive (no *to*).

### 5. All-Cleft

> I want **to leave**. → All I want **is to leave**.

---

## Decision Checklist

1. **What do you want to emphasize?** Subject, object, time, or action?
2. **For subject/object/time focus:** Use it-cleft: *It + be + focus + that/who + clause*.
3. **For action or general focus:** Use wh-cleft: *What + subject + verb + be + focus*.
4. **Use who for people, that for things and time.**
5. **In wh-clefts, use bare infinitive after be** (not *to + verb*).

### Sample 1

> It was yesterday when I met Rahul.

Formal it-cleft uses **that** for time.

> It was yesterday **that** I met Rahul.

### Sample 2

> What he did was he left early.

Wh-cleft uses bare infinitive.

> What he did was **leave** early.

## Examples That Show the Difference

1. It was **Rahul that** I met yesterday.
2. It is **honesty that** matters most.
3. It was **the noise that** woke me.
4. What I need **is** more time.
5. What she wants **is** a new phone.
6. What he did was **leave** early.
7. It was **yesterday that** I realised the truth.
8. It is **practice that** makes perfect.
9. What they need **is** better communication.
10. All I want **is to help**.

## Exam Traps and Usage Notes

### Trap 1: When in It-Clefts

> Wrong: It was yesterday **when** I met him.  
> Right: It was yesterday **that** I met him.

Use **that** (not *when*) in formal it-clefts.

### Trap 2: To-Infinitive in Wh-Clefts

> Wrong: What he did was **to leave** early.  
> Right: What he did was **leave** early.

Use bare infinitive after *be* in wh-clefts.

### Trap 3: Who vs That

> Wrong: It was Rahul **that** helped me. *(acceptable but less precise)*  
> Right: It was Rahul **who** helped me.

Use **who** for people in formal writing.

### Trap 4: Overusing Clefts

Clefts are for emphasis. Do not use them as default sentence structure. Use them when you need to highlight specific information.

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| It was yesterday **when** I met him. | It was yesterday **that** I met him. | Use *that* in it-clefts. |
| What he did was **to leave** early. | What he did was **leave** early. | Bare infinitive in wh-clefts. |

## Quick Practice

Rewrite as cleft sentences.

1. I need rest. (wh-cleft)
2. Rahul broke the window. (it-cleft, emphasize Rahul)
3. She realised the truth last night. (it-cleft, emphasize time)
4. Error spotting: It was last week when I visited them.
5. Error spotting: What she did was to apologise.
6. Fill in the blank: It was the teacher ___ explained this. (who/that)
7. Rewrite: I want peace. (wh-cleft)
8. Choose: What he needs ___ more practice. (is / are)

### Answer Key

1. **What I need is** rest.  
2. **It was Rahul who** broke the window.  
3. **It was last night that** she realised the truth.  
4. **It was last week that** I visited them.  
5. **What she did was apologise.**  
6. **who** — for people.  
7. **What I want is** peace.  
8. **is** — singular.

## Summary

> **Rule:** It-cleft = It + be + focus + that/who + clause. Wh-cleft = What + clause + be + focus (bare infinitive).

Memory trick: **It-cleft points at the star. Wh-cleft wraps the point.**

Revise these:

- It was **Rahul that** I met.  
- What I need **is** more time.  
- It was **yesterday that** I realised.  
- What he did was **leave** early.
