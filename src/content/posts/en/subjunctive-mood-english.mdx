## Introduction: "I Suggest That He Go" — Why No -s?

In formal English, certain verbs and expressions are followed by the **base form** of the verb — without *-s*, without *to*:

> I suggest that he **go** to the doctor. *(not "goes")*  
> It is important that she **be** present. *(not "is")*  
> If I **were** you, I would study harder. *(not "was")*

This is the **subjunctive mood** — one of the most distinctive features of formal English grammar.

---

## Core Meaning and Rule

> **Rule box:** The subjunctive uses the **base verb form** (no -s, no to) after verbs of demand, suggestion, and recommendation. In hypotheticals, use **were** for all persons (I were, he were, she were).

---

## Forms, Patterns, and Sentence Position

### 1. Mandative Subjunctive (Demands and Suggestions)

After verbs like *insist, suggest, recommend, demand, require, propose, ask, urge* and expressions like *it is important/necessary/essential that*, use the base verb.

> I suggest that he **study** harder. *(not "studies")*  
> She insisted that they **be** on time. *(not "are")*  
> It is essential that every student **attend** the class. *(not "attends")*  
> The doctor recommended that he **rest** for a week. *(not "rests")*  
> They demanded that the manager **apologise**. *(not "apologises")*

**American English** uses the subjunctive more consistently than British English. In British English, *should + infinitive* is often an alternative:

> BrE: I suggest that he **should study** harder. *(acceptable)*  
> AmE: I suggest that he **study** harder. *(preferred)*

### 2. Formulaic Subjunctive (Fixed Expressions)

Some fixed expressions retain the subjunctive:

> **God save** the Queen. *(not "saves")*  
> **Be** that as it may...  
> **Come** what may...  
> **Suffice it to say**...  
> **Far be it from me** to criticise...  
> If need **be**...

### 3. Were-Subjunctive (Hypotheticals)

For hypothetical or unreal situations, use **were** for all persons (I, he, she, it):

> If I **were** you, I would accept the offer.  
> If he **were** here, he would help us.  
> I wish she **were** more careful.  
> Suppose it **were** true — what would you do?  
> If it **were** not for your help, I would have failed.

**Note:** In informal English, *was* is commonly used (*If I was you...*), but in formal writing and exams, *were* is correct.

### 4. If I Were You

This is the most common use of the were-subjunctive:

> If I **were** you, I would apologise.  
> If I **were** you, I wouldn't do that.

---

## Decision Checklist

1. **Is it a demand, suggestion, or recommendation?** → Use base verb (subjunctive).
2. **Is it a fixed expression?** → Use the formulaic subjunctive.
3. **Is it a hypothetical/unreal situation?** → Use **were** for all persons.
4. **Is it formal writing or an exam?** → Prefer the subjunctive over *should + infinitive*.

## Examples That Show the Difference

1. I suggest that he **go** to the doctor. *(subjunctive — base form)*
2. It is important that she **be** present. *(subjunctive — base form)*
3. If I **were** you, I would study. *(were-subjunctive)*
4. **God save** the Queen. *(formulaic subjunctive)*
5. She insisted that they **arrive** on time. *(subjunctive)*
6. I wish he **were** here. *(were-subjunctive)*
7. The teacher recommended that she **read** more. *(subjunctive)*
8. If it **were** not for the rain, we would go. *(were-subjunctive)*
9. It is essential that every member **vote**. *(subjunctive)*
10. Suppose he **were** wrong — what then? *(were-subjunctive)*

## Exam Traps and Usage Notes

### Trap 1: "I Suggest That He Goes"

> ❌ I suggest that he **goes**.  
> ✅ I suggest that he **go**.

After *suggest, insist, recommend*, use the base form.

### Trap 2: "If I Was You"

> ❌ If I **was** you, I would study. *(informal)*  
> ✅ If I **were** you, I would study. *(formal/exam)*

### Trap 3: "God Saves the King"

> ❌ God **saves** the King.  
> ✅ God **save** the King.

This is a fixed subjunctive expression.

### Trap 4: "It Is Important That She Is"

> ❌ It is important that she **is** present.  
> ✅ It is important that she **be** present.

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| I suggest that he goes. | I suggest that he **go**. | Subjunctive = base form. |
| If I was you. | If I **were** you. | Were-subjunctive. |
| God saves the king. | God **save** the king. | Formulaic subjunctive. |
| It is important that she is. | It is important that she **be**. | Subjunctive = base form. |

## Summary

> **Rule:** Use the **base verb** after demands/suggestions (*suggest that he go*). Use **were** for all persons in hypotheticals (*If I were you*). Fixed expressions retain the subjunctive (*God save the Queen*).

Memory trick: **"Suggest, insist, demand — base verb at hand. Hypothetical? Use 'were.' Fixed expressions? Subjunctive for sure."**

Revise these:

- I suggest that he **go**. *(subjunctive)*  
- If I **were** you, I would study. *(were-subjunctive)*  
- **God save** the Queen. *(formulaic)*  
- It is essential that she **be** present. *(subjunctive)*
