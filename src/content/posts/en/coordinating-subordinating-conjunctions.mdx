## Introduction: The Glue That Holds Sentences Together

Conjunctions connect words, phrases, and clauses. English has three types, and each follows different rules.

> I was tired, **but** I worked. *(coordinating — equal clauses)*  
> **Although** he was tired, he worked. *(subordinating — dependent clause)*  
> **Neither** he **nor** she came. *(correlative — paired)*

Mixing them up creates errors. This article maps all three clearly.

---

## Core Meaning and Rule

> **Rule box:** **Coordinate** equal units with FANBOYS. **Subordinate** dependent clauses with subordinators. **Correlative** pairs must be parallel.

| Type | Function | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Coordinating | Connect equal words/clauses | for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so (FANBOYS) |
| Subordinating | Introduce dependent clauses | because, although, if, when, while, unless, so that |
| Correlative | Work in pairs | either...or, neither...nor, not only...but also, both...and |

---

## Forms, Patterns, and Sentence Position

### 1. Coordinating Conjunctions (FANBOYS)

> I was tired, **but** I continued.  
> She studied hard, **so** she passed.  
> You can have tea **or** coffee.

Coordinating conjunctions connect two independent clauses. Use a comma before the conjunction.

### 2. Subordinating Conjunctions

> **Although** he was tired, he worked.  
> I stayed home **because** it was raining.  
> **If** you study, you will pass.

Subordinating conjunctions introduce a dependent (subordinate) clause. The clause cannot stand alone.

**Common subordinators:**
- Cause: because, since, as
- Contrast: although, though, even though, while
- Condition: if, unless, provided that
- Time: when, while, before, after, until, as soon as
- Purpose: so that, in order that
- Result: so...that, such...that

### 3. Correlative Conjunctions

> **Either** you **or** she must go.  
> **Neither** he **nor** I was present.  
> **Not only** does she sing, **but** she also dances.  
> **Both** Rahul **and** Priya came.

Correlative conjunctions work in pairs. The structures after each part must be **parallel**.

> Wrong: **Not only** she sings **but also** dances.  
> Right: **Not only does** she sing, **but** she also dances.

### 4. Common Errors with Conjunctions

**Double conjunction:**
> Wrong: **Although** he was tired **but** he worked.  
> Right: **Although** he was tired, he worked. / He was tired, **but** he worked.

**Neither...or:**
> Wrong: **Neither** he **or** she came.  
> Right: **Neither** he **nor** she came.

**Because of + clause:**
> Wrong: **Because of** he was ill.  
> Right: **Because** he was ill. / **Because of** his illness.

## Decision Checklist

1. **Are you connecting two equal clauses?** → Coordinating (FANBOYS).
2. **Are you adding a dependent clause?** → Subordinating.
3. **Are you using a pair?** → Correlative.
4. **Check for double conjunctions.** Don't use *although...but* or *because...so*.
5. **Check parallelism** with correlatives.

## Examples That Show the Difference

1. I was tired, **but** I worked. *(coordinating)*
2. **Although** I was tired, I worked. *(subordinating)*
3. **Either** you **or** I must go. *(correlative)*
4. She studied hard, **so** she passed. *(coordinating)*
5. **Because** it rained, the match was cancelled. *(subordinating)*
6. **Not only** is she smart, **but** she is also kind. *(correlative)*
7. I will come **if** you invite me. *(subordinating)*
8. **Both** Rahul **and** Priya were present. *(correlative)*
9. He was tired, **yet** he continued. *(coordinating)*
10. **Unless** you hurry, you will miss the bus. *(subordinating)*

## Exam Traps and Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Although...But

> Wrong: **Although** he was tired **but** he worked.  
> Right: **Although** he was tired, he worked.

Use one or the other, not both.

### Trap 2: Neither...Or

> Wrong: **Neither** he **or** she came.  
> Right: **Neither** he **nor** she came.

The pair is *neither...nor*.

### Trap 3: Because Of + Clause

> Wrong: **Because of** he was ill.  
> Right: **Because** he was ill.

*Because of* takes a noun, not a clause.

### Trap 4: So That + Tense

> Wrong: I studied hard **so that I can** pass. *(past context)*  
> Right: I studied hard **so that I could** pass.

Match the tense of *so that* clause to the main clause.

### Trap 5: Parallelism with Correlatives

> Wrong: **Not only** she sings **but also** dances.  
> Right: **Not only does** she sing, **but** she also dances.

Keep the structures parallel after each part of the pair.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Although**...**but** | **Although**... / ..., **but**... | No double conjunction. |
| **Neither**...**or** | **Neither**...**nor** | Correct pair. |
| **Because of** he was ill. | **Because** he was ill. | *Because of* + noun. |

## Quick Practice

Choose the correct option.

1. He was tired, ___ he continued.  
   a) but b) although
2. ___ he was ill, he came to work.  
   a) But b) Although
3. ___ he ___ she was present.  
   a) Neither...or b) Neither...nor
4. I studied hard ___ I could pass.  
   a) because b) so that
5. Error spotting: Although it was raining but we went out.
6. Error spotting: Neither he or she knows the answer.
7. Error spotting: Because of she was tired.
8. Fill in the blank: Not only ___ she sing, but she also dances. (does / did)
9. Rewrite correctly: Although he tried hard but he failed.
10. Choose: You can have ___ tea ___ coffee. (either...or / neither...nor)

### Answer Key

1. **but** — coordinating.  
2. **Although** — subordinating.  
3. **Neither...nor** — correct pair.  
4. **so that** — purpose.  
5. **Although it was raining, we went out.**  
6. **Neither he nor she knows the answer.**  
7. **Because she was tired.**  
8. **does** — present tense.  
9. **Although he tried hard, he failed.** / **He tried hard, but he failed.**  
10. **either...or** — choice between two.

## Summary

> **Rule:** Coordinating = equal units (FANBOYS). Subordinating = dependent clauses. Correlative = parallel pairs. Never double up *although...but* or *because...so*.

Memory trick: **FANBOYS for equals. Subordinators for dependents. Correlatives come in pairs.**

Revise these:

- I was tired, **but** I worked. *(coordinating)*  
- **Although** I was tired, I worked. *(subordinating)*  
- **Neither** he **nor** she came. *(correlative)*  
- **Not only** does she sing, **but** she also dances. *(correlative)*
