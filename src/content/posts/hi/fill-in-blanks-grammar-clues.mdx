## Introduction: Blank कभी Random नहीं होता

Fill-in-the-blank questions simple लगते हैं, लेकिन ये guesswork नहीं होते। हर blank के आसपास grammar clues होते हैं: subject, time word, preposition pattern, या verb form जो follow करना ही होगा।

> Wrong: Neither of the answers **are** correct.  
> Right: Neither of the answers **is** correct.

**Neither** singular है। Verb agree करना चाहिए, भले ही पास में plural noun बैठा हो।

इन clues को पढ़ना सीखना fill-in-the-blanks को guesswork से systematic skill में बदल देता है। यह article बताता है कैसे।

Related strategy के लिए [How to Eliminate Wrong Options in Grammar MCQs](/hi/posts/eliminate-wrong-options-grammar-mcq/) देखें।

## Core Meaning and Rule

Fill-in-the-blank questions test करते हैं कि क्या आप missing word को control करने वाले grammar rule को identify कर सकते हैं। सबसे common control patterns ये हैं:

- **Subject-verb agreement** — verb subject से number में match करना चाहिए।
- **Tense markers** — *yesterday*, *since*, *already* जैसे time words specific tense की ओर इशारा करते हैं।
- **Collocations** — कुछ words specific partners demand करते हैं (*interested in*, *responsible for*)।
- **Sentence logic** — conditionals, comparatives और negatives predictable patterns follow करते हैं।

> **Rule box:** "जो सही लगता है" पर depend करने से पहले nearby grammar clues use करें। पहले rule खोजें, फिर word चुनें।

## Forms, Patterns, and Sentence Position

### 1. Subject-Verb Agreement Clues

Verb subject से agree करना चाहिए, nearest noun से नहीं।

> Each of the students **has** a book.

**Each** singular है, इसलिए verb **has** है, भले ही **students** plural हो।

| Subject pattern | Verb form |
|---|---|
| each / every / neither + noun | singular verb |
| either of / neither of + plural noun | singular verb (formal) |
| the number of + plural noun | singular verb |
| a number of + plural noun | plural verb |

### 2. Tense Marker Clues

Time words सबसे strong tense signals हैं।

> I have lived here **since** 2020.  
> I lived there **in** 2015.

**Since** starting point दिखाता है और usually present perfect के साथ आता है। **In 2015** finished past time है और simple past लेता है।

| Time marker | Typical tense |
|---|---|
| yesterday, last year, ago, in 2019 | simple past |
| since + point, for + duration | present perfect |
| already, yet, just, ever, never | present perfect (common) |
| by the time + past clause | past perfect |

### 3. Collocation Clues

कुछ words specific prepositions या structures demand करते हैं।

> She is interested **in** music.  
> He is responsible **for** the project.

Adjective preposition decide करता है, noun नहीं।

| Word | Required pattern |
|---|---|
| interested | in + noun |
| responsible | for + noun |
| capable | of + noun / gerund |
| accustomed | to + noun / gerund |
| different | from / than (BrE / AmE) |

### 4. Conditional Clue Patterns

Conditionals fixed verb patterns follow करते हैं। *If*-clause का tense main clause decide करता है।

> If I **were** you, I **would** apologise.

*If*-clause में past tense **were** main clause में **would** trigger करता है। यह standard second conditional pattern है।

| Conditional type | If-clause | Main clause |
|---|---|---|
| Zero (general truth) | present simple | present simple |
| First (real future) | present simple | will + base verb |
| Second (unreal present) | past simple | would + base verb |
| Third (unreal past) | past perfect | would have + past participle |

## Decision Checklist for Fill in the Blanks

हर blank के लिए यह method use करें:

1. **Identify करें कि क्या missing है।** Verb, preposition, article, conjunction, या pronoun?
2. **बाएं और दाएं देखें।** Subject क्या है? पास में कौन-सा time word है? Blank से पहले कौन-सा word आता है?
3. **Rule apply करें।** Agreement, tense, collocation, या conditional pattern?
4. **Meaning check करें।** Completed sentence logically make sense करता है?
5. **Full sentence फिर पढ़ें।** Grammar और meaning दोनों confirm करें।

### Sample 1

> Neither of the answers ___ correct.

**Neither** singular है। Verb singular होना चाहिए।

> Neither of the answers **is** correct.

### Sample 2

> I have lived here ___ 2020.

**2020** starting point है। Present perfect + starting point = **since**।

> I have lived here **since** 2020.

### Sample 3

> She is interested ___ music.

**Interested** के लिए **in** चाहिए।

> She is interested **in** music.

### Sample 4

> If I were you, I ___ apologise.

Second conditional: *if*-clause में past tense, main clause में **would**।

> If I were you, I **would** apologise.

## Examples That Show the Difference

1. Each of the boys **has** a uniform. *(singular subject)*
2. I have lived here **since** 2019. *(starting point)*
3. She **went** yesterday. *(finished past time)*
4. She is interested **in** science. *(collocation)*
5. If I **were** rich, I would travel. *(second conditional)*
6. Neither of them **is** ready. *(singular pronoun)*
7. I **have finished** already. *(present result)*
8. I **finished** it last night. *(finished past time)*
9. He is capable **of solving** this. *(collocation with gerund)*
10. By the time we arrived, the show **had started**. *(earlier past)*

हर blank visible clue से controlled है। Skill इसे देखना सीखना है।

## Exam Traps and Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Nearest Noun Trap

> Wrong: Each of the students **have** passed.  
> Right: Each of the students **has** passed.

Students plural है, लेकिन **each** subject है। Verb **each** से agree करता है।

### Trap 2: Since vs For Confusion

> Wrong: I have waited **since** three hours.  
> Right: I have waited **for** three hours.

Duration के साथ **for**, starting point के साथ **since**।

### Trap 3: Conditional Mixing

> Wrong: If I **was** you, I **will** apologise.  
> Right: If I **were** you, I **would** apologise.

Standard second conditional **were** (not *was*) और **would** (not *will*) use करता है formal grammar में।

### Trap 4: Adjective के बाद Preposition

> Wrong: She is interested **about** the topic.  
> Right: She is interested **in** the topic.

Adjective preposition fix करता है। Pair सीखें, noun नहीं।

### Trap 5: Finished Time के साथ Present Perfect

> Wrong: I **have submitted** the form yesterday.  
> Right: I **submitted** the form yesterday.

Finished past time verb को simple past की ओर ले जाता है।

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Neither of the answers **are** correct. | Neither of the answers **is** correct. | **Neither** singular है। |
| I have lived here **for** 2020. | I have lived here **since** 2020. | Starting point के साथ **since**। |
| She is interested **about** music. | She is interested **in** music. | Collocation: interested + in। |
| If I were you, I **will** apologise. | If I were you, I **would** apologise. | Second conditional pattern। |

## Quick Practice

Correct option चुनें या error correct करें।

1. Each of the members ___ present.  
   a) are b) is
2. I have worked here ___ 2018.  
   a) for b) since
3. She is responsible ___ the report.  
   a) for b) of
4. If he ___ here, he would help.  
   a) was b) were
5. Error spotting: Neither of the options are acceptable.
6. Error spotting: I have completed the task last evening.
7. Error spotting: He is interested about learning French.
8. Fill in the blank: By the time I called, she ___. (leave)
9. Rewrite correctly: Each of the girls have a notebook.
10. Choose: I have known him ___ five years. (since / for)

### Answer Key

1. **is** — **each** singular है।  
2. **since** — 2018 starting point है।  
3. **for** — responsible + for।  
4. **were** — second conditional।  
5. **Neither of the options is acceptable.**  
6. **I completed the task last evening.**  
7. **He is interested in learning French.**  
8. **had left** — earlier past action।  
9. **Each of the girls has a notebook.**  
10. **for** — five years duration है।
