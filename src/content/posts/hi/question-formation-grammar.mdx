## परिचय: "Where You Are Going?" क्यों गलत है

English में question बनाने के लिए सिर्फ question mark लगा देना काफी नहीं है। Word order बदलता है, auxiliary का सही use होता है, और indirect questions में उल्टा — question order हट जाता है।

> ❌ **Where you are going?**  
> ✅ **Where are you going?**

> ❌ **Do he play cricket?**  
> ✅ **Does he play cricket?**

इस article में चार main question types समझेंगे: **yes/no questions, wh- questions, tag questions, and indirect questions**.

---

## मूल अर्थ और नियम

> **Rule box:** Direct questions में usually **auxiliary inversion** होता है — auxiliary subject से पहले आता है। Wh- questions में wh-word पहले आता है, फिर auxiliary + subject. Tag questions statement के end में short question जोड़ते हैं। Indirect questions में **statement word order** होता है — inversion नहीं।

Memory idea:

> Direct: **Where are you going?**  
> Indirect: **Can you tell me where you are going?**

---

## 1. Yes/No Questions

Yes/no questions का answer usually **yes** या **no** से शुरू हो सकता है।

Form: **Auxiliary + Subject + Main Verb**

> **Do** you **like** tea?  
> **Can** she **swim**?  
> **Have** they **finished**?  
> **Is** he **coming**?  
> **Did** you **see** it?

अगर sentence में auxiliary नहीं है, तो **do/does/did** लगाएँ।

> ❌ **Like you** tea?  
> ✅ **Do** you **like** tea?

> ❌ **Plays he** cricket?  
> ✅ **Does** he **play** cricket?

Important: *does/did* लगने पर main verb base form में आता है।

> ✅ Does he **play** cricket?  
> ❌ Does he **plays** cricket?

---

## 2. Wh- Questions

Wh- questions information मांगते हैं — who, what, where, when, why, how, which, whose आदि।

Form: **Wh-word + Auxiliary + Subject + Main Verb**

> **Where** are you **going**?  
> **What** do you **want**?  
> **When** did they **arrive**?  
> **Why** is she **crying**?  
> **How** does it **work**?

### जब wh-word subject हो

अगर wh-word खुद subject है, तो inversion नहीं होता।

> **Who** called you? *(who = subject)*  
> **What** happened?  
> **Which** book is yours?

Compare:

> **Who did you call?** *(you = subject; who = object)*  
> **Who called you?** *(who = subject)*

---

## 3. Tag Questions

Tag question statement के end में छोटा question होता है। Tag same auxiliary use करता है, और polarity opposite होती है।

- Positive statement → negative tag
- Negative statement → positive tag

> You are coming, **aren't you**?  
> She can swim, **can't she**?  
> He doesn't like tea, **does he**?  
> They have finished, **haven't they**?  
> You won't mind, **will you**?

Tag subject से भी match करता है:

> ✅ You are coming, **aren't you**?  
> ❌ You are coming, **isn't it**?

### Special cases

> I am right, **aren't I**? *(not "amn't I")*  
> Let's go, **shall we**?  
> Nobody came, **did they**? *(nobody negative है, इसलिए positive tag)*

Words like *nobody, no one, nothing, never, hardly, scarcely* negative sense देते हैं, इसलिए tag positive होता है।

---

## 4. Indirect Questions

Indirect questions में question किसी larger sentence के अंदर होता है। ये polite या formal लगते हैं।

Important rule: **Indirect question में statement word order use होता है — subject + verb.**

> ❌ Can you tell me **where is the station**?  
> ✅ Can you tell me **where the station is**?

> ❌ Do you know **what does he want**?  
> ✅ Do you know **what he wants**?

> ❌ I wonder **when will they arrive**.  
> ✅ I wonder **when they will arrive**.

Polite forms:

> Could you tell me where the station is?  
> Do you know what time it is?  
> I wonder if you could help me.

Yes/no indirect questions में **if/whether** use होता है:

> Direct: Is he coming?  
> Indirect: Do you know **if he is coming**?

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Where you are going? | Where **are you** going? | Auxiliary inversion चाहिए। |
| Do he play? | **Does** he play? | Third person singular = does। |
| Does he plays cricket? | Does he **play** cricket? | Does के बाद base verb। |
| You are coming, isn't it? | You are coming, **aren't you**? | Tag subject/auxiliary से match करता है। |
| Can you tell me where is he? | Can you tell me where **he is**? | Indirect = statement order। |

---

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Wh-question में subject-auxiliary inversion भूलना

> ❌ Where you are going?  
> ✅ Where are you going?

### Trap 2: *do/does/did* के बाद verb बदलना

> ✅ Did she call you?  
> ❌ Did she called you?

### Trap 3: Indirect question को direct question जैसा बनाना

> ✅ I wonder why he left.  
> ❌ I wonder why did he leave.

### Trap 4: Tag में "isn't it" हर जगह लगाना

> ❌ You know him, isn't it?  
> ✅ You know him, don't you?

## Quick Practice

Correct option चुनें या error spot करें।

1. ___ you like coffee? *(Do/Does)*
2. Where ___ going? *(you are/are you)*
3. She can swim, ___? *(can she/can't she)*
4. Error spotting: Do he play cricket?
5. Error spotting: Where you are going?
6. Error spotting: You are coming, isn't it?
7. Error spotting: Can you tell me where is the station?
8. Fill in: What ___ you want? *(do/does)*
9. Fill in: I wonder ___ he will come. *(when/when will)*
10. Choose: Nobody came, ___? *(did they/didn't they)*

### Answer Key

1. **Do** — second person।
2. **are you** — auxiliary inversion।
3. **can't she** — positive statement, negative tag।
4. **Does he play cricket?** — third person = does।
5. **Where are you going?** — inversion।
6. **You are coming, aren't you?** — tag matches “you are.”
7. **Can you tell me where the station is?** — statement order।
8. **do** — second person।
9. **when** — indirect question में inversion नहीं।
10. **did they** — nobody negative sense देता है।

---

## Summary

> **Rule:** Yes/no questions: auxiliary + subject. Wh- questions: wh-word + auxiliary + subject. Tags: auxiliary और subject match करें, polarity flip करें। Indirect questions: statement word order — no inversion.

Memory trick: **Direct questions invert. Wh- words lead. Tags match and flip. Indirect questions don't invert.**
