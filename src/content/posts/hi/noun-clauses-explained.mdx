## परिचय: जब एक Clause Noun का काम करता है

Noun एक शब्द है: *happiness, Delhi, teacher*। Noun phrase शब्दों का समूह है: *the tall man from Delhi*। **Noun clause** एक पूरा clause है जो noun की तरह काम करता है।

> **What he said** surprised everyone.

Clause *what he said* sentence का subject है। यह सवाल का जवाब देता है *what surprised everyone?* — बिल्कुल एक noun की तरह।

Noun clauses English grammar की सबसे ज़रूरी structures में से हैं, और ये exams और formal writing दोनों में बार-बार आते हैं। यह article इन्हें कैसे पहचानें, ये कैसे काम करते हैं, और सबसे आम errors कैसे बचें — ये समझाता है।

---

## मूल अर्थ और नियम

> **Rule box:** Noun clause एक dependent clause है जो noun की तरह काम करता है — subject, object, complement या appositive की तरह। यह अक्शर **that, whether, if, what, why, how, when, where, who, whoever, whatever** से शुरू होता है।

---

## रूप, पैटर्न और वाक्य में स्थिति

### 1. Noun Clauses as Subjects

जब noun clause subject होता है, तो verb से पहले आता है।

> **What he said** is true.  
> **Whether he comes** doesn't matter.  
> **That she passed** is wonderful news.  
> **How he escapes** remains a mystery.

**Note:** जब noun clause subject होता है, तो verb आमतौर पर singular होता है।

> ❌ **What he said are** true.  
> ✅ **What he said is** true.

### 2. Noun Clauses as Objects

Noun clause verb का object हो सकता है।

> I know **that he is honest**.  
> She asked **whether I was coming**.  
> I don't understand **what you mean**.  
> He explained **how the machine works**.  
> Do you know **where she lives**?

### 3. Noun Clauses as Object of a Preposition

> I am sure **that he will come**.  
> She was surprised **by what he said**.  
> We are thinking about **whether we should go**.  
> He insisted **that he was right**.

### 4. Noun Clauses as Subject Complements

Linking verbs (*be, seem, become*) के बाद, noun clause complement की तरह काम कर सकता है।

> The problem is **that we don't have enough time**.  
> The question is **whether we can afford it**.  
> That is **what I meant**.  
> The truth is **that he never tried**.

### 5. Noun Clauses as Appositives

Noun clause किसी अन्य noun को rename या explain कर सकता है (आमतौर पर *fact, idea, news, hope, belief*)।

> The fact **that she passed** made everyone happy.  
> I have no idea **what he wants**.  
> The news **that the exam was cancelled** relieved everyone.  
> His belief **that hard work pays off** kept him going.

### 6. Noun Clauses के Common Introducers

| Introducer | Function | उदाहरण |
|---|---|---|
| **that** | Statement | I know **that he is honest**. |
| **whether** | Yes/no question | I wonder **whether she will come**. |
| **if** | Yes/no question (informal) | I wonder **if she will come**. |
| **what** | Thing(s) | I saw **what happened**. |
| **why** | Reason | I know **why he left**. |
| **how** | Manner | She explained **how it works**. |
| **when** | Time | I don't know **when he arrives**. |
| **where** | Place | Do you know **where she lives**? |
| **who** | Person | I know **who did it**. |
| **whoever** | Any person | **Whoever comes** will be welcome. |
| **whatever** | Any thing | **Whatever you decide** is fine with me. |

## Decision Checklist

1. **क्या clause "what?" का जवाब दे सकता है?** → यह noun clause हो सकता है।
2. **क्या यह verb का subject है?** → Noun clause as subject।
3. **क्या यह verb या preposition का object है?** → Noun clause as object।
4. **क्या यह linking verb के बाद आता है?** → Noun clause as complement।
5. **क्या यह noun को rename करता है?** → Noun clause as appositive।
6. **क्या यह that, whether, if, what, why, how, when, where, who से शुरू होता है?** → संभवतः noun clause।

## अंतर दिखाने वाले उदाहरण

1. **What he said** is true. *(subject)*
2. I know **that he is honest**. *(object of verb)*
3. She asked **whether I was coming**. *(object of verb)*
4. The problem is **that we don't have enough time**. *(complement)*
5. The fact **that she passed** made everyone happy. *(appositive)*
6. I don't understand **what you mean**. *(object)*
7. **Whether he comes** doesn't matter. *(subject)*
8. I am sure **that he will come**. *(object of preposition)*
9. Do you know **where she lives**? *(object)*
10. **Whoever finishes first** will get a prize. *(subject)*

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Noun Clauses में Word Order

> ❌ I know where **does he live**.  
> ✅ I know where **he lives**.

Noun clause **statement word order** (subject + verb) उपयोग करता है, question word order (auxiliary + subject + verb) नहीं। भले ही clause question word से शुरू हो, यह question नहीं है।

> ❌ Can you tell me what **is his name**?  
> ✅ Can you tell me what **his name is**?

### Trap 2: Subject-Verb Agreement

> ❌ **What he said are** true.  
> ✅ **What he said is** true.

Subject की तरह उपयोग होने वाला noun clause **singular** verb लेता है।

> ❌ **Whether they agree** don't matter.  
> ✅ **Whether they agree** doesn't matter.

### Trap 3: If vs Whether

*If* और *whether* दोनों noun clauses introduce कर सकते हैं, लेकिन formal writing और prepositions के बाद *whether* preferred है।

> ✅ I wonder **whether** she will come. *(formal)*  
> ✅ I wonder **if** she will come. *(informal)*  
> ✅ I am thinking about **whether** to go. *(preposition के बाद — formal)*  
> ❌ I am thinking about **if** to go. *(preposition के बाद — avoid)*

### Trap 4: Certain Verbs के बाद That-Clauses

कुछ verbs आमतौर पर *that*-clauses लेते हैं: *say, think, believe, know, hope, suggest, recommend, insist, agree, deny*।

> I **suggest** **that he study** harder. *(subjunctive — base form)*  
> I **believe** **that she is** honest. *(indicative — normal verb form)*

Formal English में, suggestion के verbs (*suggest, recommend, insist, demand, require*) **subjunctive** लेते हैं — verb का base form *-s* के बिना।

## आम गलतियाँ

| ग़लत | सही | क्यों |
|---|---|---|
| I know where does he live. | I know where **he lives**. | Noun clause में statement word order। |
| What he said are true. | What he said **is** true. | Noun clause subject = singular verb। |
| Whether he comes matter. | Whether he comes **matters**. | Singular verb ज़रूरी। |
| I am sure that he will come. | ✅ सही है | Standard that-clause। |

## Quick Practice

Noun clause और उसका function identify करिए या error ढूढ़िए।

1. ___ is unknown. (What he wants / What does he want)
2. I don't know ___ . (where she lives / where does she live)
3. Error spotting: I know what is his problem.
4. Error spotting: What they need are more resources.
5. Error spotting: Whether he agrees don't change anything.
6. Fill in the blank: The fact ___ he passed made everyone happy. (that / what)
7. Fill in the blank: I wonder ___ she will come. (if / whether)
8. Identify the noun clause: She explained how the system works.
9. Identify the function: **That he is honest** is well known.
10. Rewrite correctly: I want to know what does he need.

### Answer Key

1. **What he wants** — subject noun clause।  
2. **where she lives** — object noun clause।  
3. **I know what his problem is.** — statement word order।  
4. **What they need is more resources.** — singular verb।  
5. **Whether he agrees doesn't change anything.** — singular verb।  
6. **that** — appositive noun clause।  
7. **whether** — formal; *if* informal में भी मान्य।  
8. **how the system works** — *explained* का object।  
9. **Subject** — noun clause *is* का subject है।  
10. **I want to know what he needs.** — statement word order।

## सारांश

> **Rule:** Noun clause noun की तरह काम करता है — subject, object, complement या appositive। यह *that, whether, if, what, why, how, when, where, who* से शुरू होता है। **Statement word order** उपयोग करें (question order नहीं)। Subject की तरह noun clause **singular** verb लेता है।

Memory trick: **"Noun clauses 'what?' का जवाब देते हैं। ये statement order उपयोग करते हैं। ये singular verb लेते हैं। ये that, whether, what, why, how से शुरू होते हैं।"**

ये revise करें:

- **What he said** is true. *(subject)*  
- I know **that he is honest**. *(object)*  
- The problem is **that we don't have time**. *(complement)*  
- The fact **that she passed** made everyone happy. *(appositive)*
