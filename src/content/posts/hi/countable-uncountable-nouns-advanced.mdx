## परिचय: "Chicken" कब Count करती है और कब नहीं

कुछ nouns अपनी countability meaning के अनुसार बदलती हैं:

> I ate **a chicken**. *(एक पूरा पक्षी — countable)*  
> I ate **chicken**. *(माँस — uncountable)*  
> She bought **a paper**. *(अखबार — countable)*  
> She bought **some paper**. *(material — uncountable)*

यह article उन advanced rules और exceptions को cover करता है जो अच्छे English students को भी कन्फ़्यूज़ कर देती हैं — और exams में बार-बार आती हैं।

---

## मूल अर्थ और नियम

> **Rule box:** Countability **context में meaning** पर निर्भर करती है। कुछ nouns हमेशा countable हैं, कुछ हमेशा uncountable, और कुछ दोनों के बीच बदलती हैं। अपने intended meaning के अनुसार articles, plurals और quantifiers चुनें।

## रूप, पैटर्न और वाक्य में स्थिति

### 1. Nouns जो Meaning से Countability बदलती हैं

| Noun | Countable Meaning | Uncountable Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **chicken** | एक पूरा पक्षी | माँस |
| **paper** | अखबार / document | material |
| **glass** | पीने का गिलास | material |
| **iron** | इस्त्री | धातु |
| **room** | कमरा | जगह |
| **experience** | कोई specific event | सामान्य knowledge/skill |
| **coffee** | एक कप कॉफ़ी | substance |
| **hair** | अलग-अलग बाल | पूरा mass |
| **time** | कोई occasion | सामान्य concept |

> I had **two coffees**. *(दो कप — countable)*  
> I don't drink much **coffee**. *(substance — uncountable)*

> She had **an interesting experience** at the concert. *(specific event)*  
> She has **a lot of experience** in teaching. *(general skill)*

### 2. Nouns जो हमेशा Uncountable हैं (Common Errors)

ये nouns कभी *a/an* या plural में उपयोग नहीं होते:

| Noun | ग़लत | सही |
|---|---|---|
| advice | an advice, advices | a piece of advice, some advice |
| furniture | a furniture, furnitures | a piece of furniture, some furniture |
| information | an information, informations | a piece of information, some information |
| luggage | a luggage, luggages | a piece of luggage, some luggage |
| news | a news, newses | a piece of news, some news |
| homework | a homework, homeworks | some homework |
| knowledge | a knowledges | some knowledge |
| progress | a progress, progresses | some progress, much progress |

**Countable बनाने के लिए "a piece of" उपयोग करें:**

> ❌ She gave me **an advice**.  
> ✅ She gave me **a piece of advice**.

### 3. Quantifiers: Countable vs Uncountable

| Quantifier | उपयोग | उदाहरण |
|---|---|---|
| **many** | countable | many books, many students |
| **few / a few** | countable | few friends, a few ideas |
| **fewer** | countable | fewer mistakes |
| **much** | uncountable | much time, much effort |
| **little / a little** | uncountable | little hope, a little water |
| **less** | uncountable | less time, less money |
| **a lot of / lots of** | दोनों | a lot of books / a lot of time |
| **some / any** | दोनों | some books / some time |
| **enough** | दोनों | enough books / enough time |

### 4. Hair: Countable या Uncountable?

> She has long **hair**. *(uncountable — पूरा mass)*  
> There are **two hairs** in my soup. *(countable — अलग-अलग बाल)*

अधिकांश contexts में *hair* uncountable है। Countable form केवल individual strands के लिए उपयोग करें।

### 5. Nouns जो Plural दिखते हैं लेकिन Uncountable हैं

> **News** uncountable है: The news **is** good.  
> **Mathematics** uncountable है: Mathematics **is** difficult.  
> **Physics** uncountable है: Physics **is** my favourite subject.

### 6. Nouns जो Singular दिखते हैं लेकिन Plural हैं

> **Scissors** plural हैं: The scissors **are** on the table.  
> **Trousers** plural हैं: These trousers **are** too long.  
> **Glasses** (spectacles) plural हैं: My glasses **are** broken.

## Decision Checklist

1. **क्या noun countable या uncountable sense में उपयोग हो रहा है?** Meaning जाँचें।
2. **क्या यह हमेशा uncountable है?** (advice, furniture, information, luggage, news, etc.) → *a/an* नहीं, plural नहीं।
3. **कौन सा quantifier चाहिए?** Countable → many, few, fewer। Uncountable → much, little, less। दोनों → some, a lot of, enough।
4. **क्या noun plural दिखता है लेकिन singular act करता है?** (news, mathematics, physics) → Singular verb।
5. **क्या noun singular दिखता है लेकिन plural act करता है?** (scissors, trousers, glasses) → Plural verb।

## अंतर दिखाने वाले उदाहरण

1. I ate **a chicken**. *(पक्षी — countable)* vs I ate **chicken**. *(माँस — uncountable)*
2. She gave me **a piece of advice**. *(not "an advice")*
3. There is **much furniture** in the room. *(not "many furniture")*
4. **Two coffees**, please. *(कप — countable)*
5. The news **is** shocking. *(uncountable — singular verb)*
6. These scissors **are** sharp. *(plural)*
7. She has **a lot of experience**. *(uncountable — skill)*
8. There are **three rooms** in the house. *(bedrooms — countable)*
9. There is **no room** for more. *(space — uncountable)*
10. I need **some information**. *(not "an information")*

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### Trap 1: "An Advice" / "Advices"

> ❌ She gave me **an advice**.  
> ✅ She gave me **a piece of advice** / **some advice**.

### Trap 2: "Many Furniture"

> ❌ There is **many furniture** in the room.  
> ✅ There is **much furniture** / **a lot of furniture** in the room.

### Trap 3: "A News"

> ❌ This is **a good news**.  
> ✅ This is **good news**. / This is **a piece of good news**.

### Trap 4: "Two Coffees" — क्या यह सही है?

> ✅ Can I have **two coffees**, please? *(informal लेकिन व्यापक रूप से मान्य — "two cups of coffee" का अर्थ)*

Restaurants और cafés में *two coffees* पूरी तरह मान्य है। Formal writing में *two cups of coffee* कहें।

### Trap 5: Less vs Fewer

> ❌ **Less** people came today.  
> ✅ **Fewer** people came today. *(people = countable)*

> ❌ **Fewer** time is needed.  
> ✅ **Less** time is needed. *(time = uncountable)*

## सारांश

> **Rule:** Countability meaning पर निर्भर करती है। कुछ nouns countable और uncountable के बीच बदलती हैं। हमेशा uncountable nouns (advice, furniture, information, news, luggage) कभी *a/an* या plural नहीं लेते। Countable के लिए *many/fewer*, uncountable के लिए *much/less* उपयोग करें।

Memory trick: **"Advice, furniture, news — a/an नहीं, plural नहीं। Chicken, paper, coffee — meaning पर निर्भर करता है। Countable के लिए many। Uncountable के लिए much।"**

ये revise करें:

- She gave me **a piece of advice**. *(not "an advice")*  
- There is **much furniture** in the room. *(not "many furniture")*  
- **Two coffees**, please. *(कप — countable)*  
- The news **is** good. *(uncountable — singular verb)*  
- These scissors **are** sharp. *(plural)*
