## Introduction: एक ही Verb, Two Different Meanings

कुछ verbs gerund (*-ing*) और infinitive (*to + verb*) दोनों ले सकते हैं। यह choice free नहीं है — इससे meaning बदल जाता है।

> I **stopped smoking**. *(मैंने छोड़ दिया।)*  
> I **stopped to smoke**. *(मैंने smoke करने के लिए रुका।)*

Verb **stop** दोनों forms लेता है, लेकिन meaning पूरी तरह अलग है। यह article आपको सिखाता है कि कौन-से verbs कौन-सा form लेते हैं, और दोनों possible हों तो क्या होता है।

## Core Meaning and Rule

एक **gerund** (*-ing* form जो noun की तरह use होता है) और एक **infinitive** (*to + base verb*) दोनों verbs के बाद आ सकते हैं, लेकिन pattern specific verb पर depend करता है।

- कुछ verbs **सिर्फ gerunds** लेते हैं: *enjoy, avoid, mind, finish, practise, consider*।
- कुछ verbs **सिर्फ infinitives** लेते हैं: *decide, plan, hope, want, refuse, manage, promise*।
- कुछ verbs **दोनों लेते हैं, लेकिन meaning बदलता है**: *stop, remember, forget, try, regret, go on*।

> **Rule box:** Key verbs के बाद patterns याद करें। जब दोनों possible हों, meaning से choose करें।

## Forms, Patterns, and Sentence Position

### 1. Gerund लेने वाले Verbs

> I **enjoy reading**. *(not "enjoy to read")*  
> She **avoided meeting** him.  
> Have you **finished writing**?

| Verb | Example |
|---|---|
| enjoy | I enjoy **swimming**. |
| avoid | He avoided **answering**. |
| mind | Do you mind **waiting**? |
| finish | She finished **cooking**. |
| practise | We practised **speaking**. |
| consider | They considered **moving**. |
| suggest | I suggest **leaving** early. |
| deny | He denied **cheating**. |

### 2. Infinitive लेने वाले Verbs

> She **decided to leave**. *(not "decided leaving")*  
> He **planned to visit** Delhi.  
> They **hope to win**.

| Verb | Example |
|---|---|
| decide | She decided **to stay**. |
| plan | We plan **to travel**. |
| hope | I hope **to pass**. |
| want | He wants **to learn**. |
| refuse | She refused **to go**. |
| manage | They managed **to escape**. |
| promise | He promised **to help**. |
| afford | We cannot afford **to wait**. |

### 3. Meaning बदलने वाले Verbs

ये सबसे important हैं सीखने के लिए क्योंकि gerund/infinitive choice sentence का meaning बदल देती है।

**Stop:**
> I **stopped smoking**. *(मैंने छोड़ दिया।)*  
> I **stopped to smoke**. *(मैंने smoke करने के लिए रुका।)*

**Remember:**
> I **remember locking** the door. *(मुझे याद है मैंने lock किया था।)*  
> I **remember to lock** the door. *(मैं भूलता नहीं कि lock करना है।)*

**Forget:**
> I'll never **forget meeting** her. *(मैंने मिला; याद बनी रहेगी।)*  
> I **forgot to meet** her. *(मैं नहीं गया; भूल गया।)*

**Try:**
> I **tried opening** the window. *(मैंने यह action experiment की।)*  
> I **tried to open** the window. *(मैंने try किया; success uncertain।)*

**Regret:**
> I **regret telling** him. *(मैंने बताया; अब मुझे regret है।)*  
> I **regret to tell** you. *(मुझे inform करते हुए regret है।)*

**Go on:**
> She **went on talking** for an hour. *(वह वही action continue करती रही।)*  
> She **went on to become** a doctor. *(कुछ और के बाद, उसने यह achieve किया।)*

## Decision Checklist

1. **Main verb identify करें।** क्या gerund verb है, infinitive verb, या dual-meaning verb?
2. **अगर gerund-only:** *-ing* use करें।
3. **अगर infinitive-only:** *to + base verb* use करें।
4. **अगर dual-meaning:** intended meaning check करें।
   - Past action की memory → gerund
   - Duty या future action → infinitive
   - Purpose → infinitive
   - Cessation → gerund
5. **Completed sentence पढ़ें।** क्या meaning match करता है?

### Sample 1

> I enjoy to read.

**Enjoy** सिर्फ gerund लेता है।

> I enjoy **reading**.

### Sample 2

> She decided going.

**Decide** सिर्फ infinitive लेता है।

> She decided **to go**.

### Sample 3

> I stopped to smoke.

इसका मतलब "मैंने smoke करने के लिए रुका।" अगर आपका मतलब "मैंने छोड़ दिया":

> I stopped **smoking**.

## Examples That Show the Difference

1. I enjoy **reading** novels. *(gerund only)*
2. She decided **to leave**. *(infinitive only)*
3. I stopped **smoking**. *(quit the habit)*
4. I stopped **to smoke**. *(paused for smoking)*
5. I remember **locking** the door. *(past memory)*
6. Remember **to lock** the door. *(don't forget)*
7. I forgot **to call** her. *(I didn't call.)*
8. I'll never **forget visiting** Paris. *(memory of the trip)*
9. She tried **to open** the jar. *(attempted)*
10. She tried **adding** salt. *(experimented)*

## Exam Traps and Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Enjoy + Infinitive

> Wrong: I enjoy **to read**.  
> Right: I enjoy **reading**.

**Enjoy** हमेशा gerund लेता है।

### Trap 2: Decide + Gerund

> Wrong: She decided **going**.  
> Right: She decided **to go**.

**Decide** हमेशा infinitive लेता है।

### Trap 3: Stop + Gerund और Stop + Infinitive को Confuse करना

> I stopped **smoking**. *(मैंने छोड़ दिया।)*  
> I stopped **to smoke**. *(मैंने smoke करने के लिए रुका।)*

Meaning opposite है। Difference सीखें।

### Trap 4: Remember + Gerund vs Remember + Infinitive

> I remember **meeting** her. *(मुझे याद है।)*  
> I remembered **to meet** her. *(मैं भूला नहीं।)*

Gerund = past memory। Infinitive = duty not forgotten।

### Trap 5: Like, Love, Hate, Prefer

ये verbs दोनों forms ले सकते हैं, meaning में बहुत कम फर्क।

> I like **swimming**. = I like **to swim**.

हालाँकि specific occasions के लिए infinitive more common है:

> I'd like **to go** now. *(not "I'd like going now")*

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| I enjoy **to read**. | I enjoy **reading**. | Enjoy + gerund. |
| She decided **going**. | She decided **to go**. | Decide + infinitive. |
| I stopped **to smoke**. *(meaning quit)* | I stopped **smoking**. | Stop + gerund = quit. |
| I forgot **meeting** her. *(meaning didn't go)* | I forgot **to meet** her. | Forget + infinitive = failed to remember. |

## Quick Practice

1. I enjoy ___ in the morning.  
   a) to run b) running
2. She decided ___ the course.  
   a) joining b) to join
3. I stopped ___ last year. *(quit)*  
   a) to smoke b) smoking
4. Remember ___ the door. *(don't forget)*  
   a) locking b) to lock
5. Error spotting: I enjoy to watch movies.
6. Error spotting: He decided going home early.
7. Error spotting: I stopped to rest. *(meaning: I paused for rest — is this correct?)*
8. Fill in the blank: I forgot ___ her. (call / to call) — meaning: I didn't call.
9. Rewrite correctly: She enjoys to cook.
10. Choose: I remember ___ the window. (closing / to close) — meaning: I have the memory.

### Answer Key

1. **running** — enjoy + gerund.  
2. **to join** — decide + infinitive.  
3. **smoking** — stop + gerund = quit.  
4. **to lock** — remember + infinitive = don't forget.  
5. **I enjoy watching movies.**  
6. **He decided to go home early.**  
7. **Correct** — stop + infinitive = pause for purpose.  
8. **to call** — forget + infinitive = failed to remember.  
9. **She enjoys cooking.**  
10. **closing** — remember + gerund = past memory.
