## परिचय: "A Big Red Car" सही क्यों लगता है

Adjectives nouns को describe करते हैं — यह basic बात है। लेकिन problem तब आती है जब एक noun से पहले दो या तीन adjectives आ जाएँ।

> ❌ a red big car  
> ✅ a **big red car**

> ❌ an old beautiful Indian temple  
> ✅ a **beautiful old Indian temple**

English speakers adjective order को अक्सर naturally follow करते हैं। Learners और exam candidates के लिए यह rule useful है क्योंकि गलत order meaning को पूरी तरह नहीं बिगाड़ता, लेकिन sentence unnatural लगता है।

---

## मूल नियम

> **Rule box:** जब noun से पहले कई adjectives आएँ, common order होता है: **opinion → size → age → shape → colour → origin → material → purpose → noun**.

Memory chain:

> **Opinion, Size, Age, Shape, Colour, Origin, Material, Purpose, Noun**

Examples:

> a **lovely little** cottage  
> a **beautiful old Indian** temple  
> a **small round wooden** table  
> two **lovely little black** kittens

Opinion adjectives factual adjectives से पहले आते हैं:

> a **beautiful old** house  
> not: an old beautiful house

---

## Adjective Categories

| Category | Meaning | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Opinion | judgement/feeling | beautiful, lovely, ugly, useful |
| Size | size | big, small, tiny, huge |
| Age | age | old, young, new, ancient |
| Shape | shape/form | round, square, flat, narrow |
| Colour | colour | red, black, blue, golden |
| Origin | place/nationality | Indian, Japanese, Italian |
| Material | material | wooden, cotton, metal, silk |
| Purpose | use/function | sleeping bag, racing car, writing desk |

Full pattern:

> Determiner + opinion + size + age + shape + colour + origin + material + purpose + noun

हर sentence में सभी categories नहीं आतीं। Usually दो या तीन adjectives काफी होते हैं।

## Patterns और Sentence Position

### 1. Noun से पहले

Most exam questions में adjectives noun से पहले होते हैं।

> a **big red car**  
> a **small round wooden table**  
> an **expensive Italian leather bag**

### 2. Linking verb के बाद

अगर adjectives linking verb के बाद आते हैं, तो order इतना strict नहीं लगता; वे अलग-अलग qualities बताते हैं।

> The car is **big and red**.  
> The temple is **beautiful, old, and Indian**.

लेकिन noun से पहले phrase बने तो usual order follow करें:

> a **beautiful old Indian temple**

### 3. Commas कब लगते हैं?

Coordinate adjectives में comma आ सकता है:

> a **long, difficult** journey

लेकिन cumulative adjective order में generally comma नहीं लगता:

> a **beautiful old Indian** temple  
> a **small round wooden** table

Simple test: अगर adjectives के बीच **and** naturally आ सकता है, comma possible है। अगर adjectives fixed order में noun तक build कर रहे हैं, comma avoid करें।

## Decision Checklist

Exam में यह method use करें:

1. जिस noun को describe किया जा रहा है, उसे identify करें।
2. उसके पहले आने वाले adjectives list करें।
3. Categories पहचानें: opinion, size, age, shape, colour, origin, material, purpose.
4. Opinion को factual categories से पहले रखें।
5. Phrase दोबारा पढ़ें — क्या यह natural लग रहा है?

Example 1:

> red / big / car

- big = size
- red = colour

Correct:

> a **big red car**

Example 2:

> old / beautiful / Indian / temple

- beautiful = opinion
- old = age
- Indian = origin

Correct:

> a **beautiful old Indian temple**

Example 3:

> wooden / small / round / table

- small = size
- round = shape
- wooden = material

Correct:

> a **small round wooden table**

## Examples That Show the Difference

> ✅ She bought a **big red car**.  
> ❌ She bought a red big car.

> ✅ We visited a **beautiful old Indian temple**.  
> ❌ We visited an old beautiful Indian temple.

> ✅ He placed the vase on a **small round wooden table**.  
> ❌ He placed the vase on a wooden small round table.

> ✅ Two **lovely little black kittens** were sleeping.  
> ❌ Two black little lovely kittens were sleeping.

More correct examples:

> She wore an **elegant long blue dress**.  
> They live in a **large modern glass building**.  
> He bought a **new Japanese sports car**.  
> We need a **useful small reading lamp**.

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Opinion को factual adjective के बाद रखना

> ❌ an old beautiful house  
> ✅ a **beautiful old house**

Opinion usually पहले आता है।

### Trap 2: Material को size/shape से पहले रखना

> ❌ a wooden small round table  
> ✅ a **small round wooden table**

Material noun के करीब आता है, size और shape उससे पहले।

### Trap 3: Purpose word noun के सबसे पास होता है

Purpose word often noun के ठीक पास होता है:

> a **sleeping bag**  
> a **racing car**  
> a **writing desk**

अगर कोई और adjective हो, तो वह purpose word से पहले आएगा:

> a **new sleeping bag**  
> a **fast racing car**

### Usage note: बहुत ज्यादा adjectives न लगाएँ

Long adjective strings technically possible हैं, लेकिन writing heavy हो जाती है।

> a beautiful small old round red Italian wooden writing desk

Order सही हो सकता है, लेकिन style खराब लगेगी। Better writing में phrase को छोटा करें या separate sentence use करें।

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| a red big car | a **big red car** | Size before colour। |
| an old beautiful Indian temple | a **beautiful old Indian temple** | Opinion → age → origin। |
| a wooden small round table | a **small round wooden table** | Size → shape → material। |
| two black little lovely kittens | two **lovely little black kittens** | Opinion → size → colour। |

## Quick Practice

Correct adjective order चुनें।

1. a ___ car *(red big / big red)*
2. a ___ temple *(beautiful old Indian / Indian old beautiful)*
3. a ___ table *(small round wooden / wooden round small)*
4. two ___ kittens *(lovely little black / black little lovely)*
5. Error spotting: She bought a red big car.
6. Error spotting: We saw an old beautiful Indian temple.
7. Error spotting: He used a wooden small round table.
8. Rewrite correctly: black / little / lovely / kittens
9. Fill in: a ___ bag *(new sleeping / sleeping new)*
10. Choose: an ___ dress *(elegant long blue / blue long elegant)*

### Answer Key

1. **big red** — size before colour।
2. **beautiful old Indian** — opinion → age → origin।
3. **small round wooden** — size → shape → material।
4. **lovely little black** — opinion → size → colour।
5. **She bought a big red car.**
6. **We saw a beautiful old Indian temple.**
7. **He used a small round wooden table.**
8. **lovely little black kittens**
9. **new sleeping** — ordinary adjective purpose word से पहले।
10. **elegant long blue** — opinion → size/length → colour।

## Summary

> **Rule:** Adjective order usually follows **opinion → size → age → shape → colour → origin → material → purpose → noun**. Opinion factual adjectives से पहले आता है; material और purpose noun के करीब रहते हैं।

Memory trick: **OSASCOMP** — Opinion, Size, Age, Shape, Colour, Origin, Material, Purpose.

Revision examples:

> a **big red car**  
> a **beautiful old Indian temple**  
> a **small round wooden table**
