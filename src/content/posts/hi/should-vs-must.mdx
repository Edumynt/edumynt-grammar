## परिचय: Advice और Obligation बराबर नहीं हैं

ये दोनों sentences grammatical हैं, लेकिन meaning की strength अलग है:

> You **should** wear a helmet.

> You **must** wear a helmet.

पहला sentence advice या recommendation देता है। दूसरा strong necessity, rule, duty या obligation बताता है। इसलिए "should" और "must" दोनों modal verbs होने के बावजूद बहुत अलग meaning बनाते हैं। Exams, official instructions और writing में यह difference खास important है।

---

## Core Meaning और Rule

- **Should** = advice, recommendation, expectation, या moral suggestion.
- **Must** = strong necessity, obligation, rule, duty, या confident deduction.

> **Rule box:** **Should** weaker advice है। **Must** stronger necessity है।

Compare करें:

> You **should** try this restaurant.  
> You **must** wear a helmet by law.

Restaurant try करना suggestion है। Helmet पहनना law के कारण obligation है।

---

## Forms, Patterns और Sentence Position

"Should" और "must" modal verbs हैं। इनके साथ he/she/it में -s नहीं लगता, और इनके बाद main verb का base form आता है।

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| subject + should/must + base verb | She **should rest**. / She **must leave** now. |
| should/must + subject + base verb | **Should** we wait? / **Must** I sign here? |
| subject + should/must + not + base verb | You **should not ignore** this. / You **must not enter**. |

गलत: ~~He must goes now.~~  
सही: He **must go** now.

### Main Uses

| Meaning | Better Modal | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Advice | should | You **should** revise your answers. |
| Mild duty | should | We **should** help people in need. |
| Rule/law | must | Passengers **must** show their tickets. |
| Urgent necessity | must | We **must** act quickly. |
| Confident deduction | must | He **must** be at home; the lights are on. |
| Negative advice | should not | You **should not** skip breakfast. |
| Prohibition | must not | You **must not** park here. |

## Decision Checklist

Blank या error-spotting sentence में यह पूछें:

1. Sentence advice या recommendation दे रहा है? **Should**.
2. Rule, law, या unavoidable duty है? **Must**.
3. Speaker evidence से strong conclusion निकाल रहा है? **Must**.
4. Negative meaning "यह अच्छा idea नहीं है" है? **Should not**.
5. Negative meaning "allowed नहीं है" है? **Must not**.

### Checklist Examples

> You ___ consult a doctor if the pain continues.

यह advice है। Correct: You **should** consult a doctor.

> All visitors ___ carry an identity card.

यह rule है। Correct: All visitors **must** carry an identity card.

> He ___ be tired; he has worked for twelve hours.

यह strong deduction है। Correct: He **must** be tired.

## Difference दिखाने वाले Examples

1. You **should** try this restaurant.  
   Recommendation है, obligation नहीं।

2. You **must** wear a helmet by law.  
   Legal obligation.

3. I **should** study more.  
   अपने लिए advice; force weaker है।

4. I **must** study more.  
   Strong personal duty या urgent necessity.

5. He **must** be at home; the lights are on.  
   Confident deduction.

6. You **should not** speak rudely.  
   Negative advice.

7. You **must not** touch this wire.  
   Prohibition; allowed नहीं है और dangerous भी हो सकता है।

8. We **should** leave early to avoid traffic.  
   Sensible suggestion.

9. Candidates **must** answer all compulsory questions.  
   Exam instruction.

10. The answer **should** be correct, but let us check it once.  
   Expectation है, थोड़ी uncertainty के साथ।

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### 1. Ordinary Suggestions के लिए "Must" Heavy हो सकता है

Exam meaning में गलत/too strong: ~~You **must** try this restaurant.~~  
Better: You **should** try this restaurant.

Casual speech में "You must try this" enthusiastic recommendation हो सकता है, लेकिन grammar question में "must" usually strong necessity दिखाता है। Advice हो तो **should** चुनें।

### 2. Rules और Laws में Stronger Modal चाहिए

गलत: ~~You **should** wear a helmet by law.~~  
सही: You **must** wear a helmet by law.

"By law" obligation signal करता है, mild advice नहीं।

### 3. Confident Deduction में "Must"

गलत: ~~He **should** be at home; the lights are on.~~  
सही: He **must** be at home; the lights are on.

Speaker evidence से strong conclusion निकाल रहा है।

### 4. "Must Not" और "Do Not Have To" Same नहीं हैं

- **Must not** = prohibited / allowed नहीं।
- **Do not have to** = necessary नहीं।

> You **must not** submit two forms. = यह forbidden है।  
> You **do not have to** submit two forms. = एक form काफी है।

यह exam का बड़ा trap है।

### 5. Past Meaning

Past obligation के लिए English अक्सर **had to** use करती है, "musted" नहीं।

> Present: I **must** finish this today.  
> Past: I **had to** finish it yesterday.

Past deduction के लिए **must have + past participle**:

> He **must have forgotten** the meeting.

## Common Mistakes

| गलत | सही | कारण |
|---|---|---|
| You **must** try this restaurant. | You **should** try this restaurant. | Ordinary recommendation. |
| You **should** wear a helmet by law. | You **must** wear a helmet by law. | Legal obligation. |
| He **should** be at home; the lights are on. | He **must** be at home; the lights are on. | Strong deduction. |
| You **must not** come if you are busy. | You **do not have to** come if you are busy. | Not necessary, not prohibited. |
| She **must goes** now. | She **must go** now. | Modal + base verb. |
| I **should** finish this today; it is compulsory. | I **must** finish this today; it is compulsory. | Compulsion needs must. |

## Quick Practice

सही option चुनिए।

1. You ___ see a dentist if your tooth hurts. (should/must)
2. Drivers ___ stop at a red light. (should/must)
3. He ___ be the new manager; everyone is greeting him. (should/must)
4. You ___ not smoke in the hospital. (should/must)

Error identify करके correct कीजिए।

5. She musts submit the report today.
6. You should wear a seat belt; it is required by law.
7. You must not attend the optional workshop if you are busy.

Rewrite या fill in the blank.

8. "I should finish this before midnight" को stronger बनाइए।
9. You ___ not waste so much time. (should/must)
10. They ___ have missed the train; they are still at the station. (should/must)

### Answer Key

1. **should** — advice.  
2. **must** — traffic rule/law.  
3. **must** — strong deduction.  
4. **must** — prohibition.  
5. She **must submit** the report today.  
6. You **must** wear a seat belt; it is required by law.  
7. You **do not have to** attend the optional workshop if you are busy.  
8. I **must** finish this before midnight.  
9. **should** — negative advice.  
10. **must** — must have + past participle for deduction.

## Summary

> **Should** = advice, recommendation, या expectation.  
> **Must** = strong necessity, rule, duty, या confident deduction.

Memory trick: **Should suggests; must insists.**

Revision examples:

> You **should** try this restaurant.  
> You **must** wear a helmet by law.  
> He **must** be at home; the lights are on.
