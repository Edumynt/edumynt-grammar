## परिचय: "Turn It Off" क्यों और "Turn Off It" क्यों नहीं

Phrasal verbs English में हर जगह हैं:

> Please **turn off** the lights.  
> She **gave up** smoking.  
> The plane **took off** on time.  
> He **looks after** his younger brother.

लेकिन इनका grammar tricky है। हम **turn it off** कहते हैं लेकिन **turn off it** क्यों नहीं? हम **look after the child** कह सकते हैं लेकिन **look the child after** क्यों नहीं? यह article नियम समझाता है।

---

## मूल अर्थ और नियम

> **Rule box:** Phrasal verb verb + particle (adverb/preposition) का combination है। **Transitive** phrasal verbs object लेते हैं। **Intransitive** नहीं लेते। **Separable** phrasal verbs object को verb और particle के बीच रखने देते हैं। **Inseparable** नहीं देते। Pronouns के साथ, separable phrasal verbs पronoun को **ज़रूर** verb और particle के बीच रखते हैं।

---

## रूप, पैटर्न और वाक्य में स्थिति

### 1. Transitive vs Intransitive

**Transitive** phrasal verbs object लेते हैं:

> Please **turn off** the lights.  
> She **gave up** smoking.  
> He **picked up** his bag.

**Intransitive** phrasal verbs object नहीं लेते:

> The plane **took off**.  
> She **showed up** late.  
> He **grew up** in Delhi.

### 2. Separable Phrasal Verbs

**Separable** phrasal verbs के साथ, object verb और particle के बीच या particle के बाद आ सकता है।

> Please **turn off** the lights. = Please **turn** the lights **off**.  
> She **picked up** her bag. = She **picked** her bag **up**.  
> He **turned down** the offer. = He **turned** the offer **down**.

**Pronoun rule:** जब object pronoun है (*it, them, him, her*), तो यह **ज़रूर** verb और particle के बीच जाएगा।

> ✅ Please **turn it off**.  
> ❌ Please **turn off it**.

> ✅ She **picked it up**.  
> ❌ She **picked up it**.

### 3. Inseparable Phrasal Verbs

**Inseparable** phrasal verbs के साथ, object हमेशा particle के **बाद** आता है — कभी बीच में नहीं।

> She **looks after** her brother.  
> ❌ She **looks** her brother **after**.

> He **ran into** an old friend.  
> ❌ He **ran** an old friend **into**.

यह rule pronouns पर भी लागू होता है:

> ✅ She **looks after him**.  
> ❌ She **looks him after**.

### 4. Three-Part Phrasal Verbs

कुछ phrasal verbs में तीन parts होते हैं: verb + adverb + preposition। ये हमेशा inseparable होते हैं।

> She **looks up to** her teacher. *(admire करना)*  
> He **gets along with** everyone. *(अच्छा रिश्ता होना)*  
> We **look forward to** the holidays. *(उत्सा से इंतज़ार करना)*  
> She **comes up with** new ideas. *(नए ideas सोचना)*

> ✅ She **looks up to her**.  
> ❌ She **looks her up to**.

**Note:** *Look forward to* के बाद noun या **-ing** form आती है, infinitive नहीं।

> ✅ I look forward to **meeting** you.  
> ❌ I look forward to **meet** you.

### 5. Common Phrasal Verbs

**Separable:**
- turn off / turn the light off
- pick up / pick the book up
- turn down / turn the offer down
- put off / put the meeting off
- bring up / bring the topic up
- figure out / figure the answer out
- look up / look the word up

**Inseparable:**
- look after / look after the child
- run into / run into an old friend
- get over / get over an illness
- deal with / deal with a problem
- depend on / depend on someone

**Intransitive:**
- take off / The plane took off.
- show up / She showed up late.
- grow up / He grew up in Mumbai.
- break down / The car broke down.

## Decision Checklist

1. **क्या phrasal verb object लेता है?** → हाँ: transitive। नहीं: intransitive।
2. **क्या यह separable है या inseparable?** → List देखें या test करें: क्या object बीच में आ सकता है?
3. **क्या object pronoun है?** → अगर separable, तो pronoun **ज़रूर** verb और particle के बीच।
4. **क्या यह three-part phrasal verb है?** → हमेशा inseparable। Object अंत में।
5. **क्या particle preposition है?** → आमतौर पर inseparable। Object preposition के बाद।

## अंतर दिखाने वाले उदाहरण

1. Please **turn off** the lights. = Please **turn** the lights **off**.
2. Please **turn it off**. *(pronoun — बीच में ज़रूर)*
3. She **looks after** her brother. *(inseparable)*
4. The plane **took off**. *(intransitive)*
5. He **turned down** the offer. = He **turned** the offer **down**.
6. She **ran into** an old friend. *(inseparable)*
7. I **look forward to** **meeting** you. *(three-part, inseparable, + -ing)*
8. He **gave up** smoking. *(transitive, gerund object)*
9. She **picked up** the book. = She **picked** the book **up**.
10. The car **broke down**. *(intransitive)*

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Pronoun Placement

> ❌ Turn off it.  
> ✅ Turn **it** off.

Separable phrasal verbs के साथ, pronouns verb और particle के बीच जाते हैं।

### Trap 2: Inseparable Verbs with Pronouns

> ❌ Look him after.  
> ✅ Look after **him**.

Inseparable phrasal verbs pronoun को particle के बाद रखते हैं।

### Trap 3: Look Forward To + -ing

> ❌ I look forward to **meet** you.  
> ✅ I look forward to **meeting** you.

*Look forward to* के बाद noun या -ing form, infinitive नहीं।

### Trap 4: Give Up + Gerund

> ✅ He **gave up** smoking.  
> ✅ He **gave** smoking **up**. *(noun object के साथ separable)*

*Ggive up* separable है, लेकिन जब object gerund हो, तो यह आमतौर पर particle के बाद रहता है।

### Trap 5: Similar Phrasal Verbs को कन्फ़्यूज़ करना

> **Look into** = investigate करना  
> **Look after** = देखभाल करना  
> **Look up to** = admire करना  
> **Look down on** = तुच्छ समझना  
> **Look forward to** = उत्सा से इंतज़ार करना

## सारांश

> **Rule:** Separable phrasal verbs object को verb और particle के बीच रखने देते हैं। Pronouns **ज़रूर** बीच में जाते हैं। Inseparable phrasal verbs object को particle के बाद रखते हैं। Three-part phrasal verbs हमेशा inseparable होते हैं।

Memory trick: **"Pronouns separable को तोड़ते हैं। Inseparable साथ रहते हैं। तीन parts कभी नहीं टूटते।"**

ये revise करें:

- Please **turn it off**. *(separable, pronoun बीच में)*  
- She **looks after** him. *(inseparable)*  
- The plane **took off**. *(intransitive)*  
- I look forward to **meeting** you. *(three-part, + -ing)*
