## Introduction: जब Action Doer से ज़्यादा Matter करता है

Active voice में subject action करता है। Passive voice में subject action receive करता है।

> Active: They **built** the bridge in 1990.  
> Passive: The bridge **was built** in 1990.

Passive focus को doer से action या उसके receiver की तरफ shift करता है। यह academic writing, news reports और formal contexts में common है।

यह article आपको हर tense में passive बनाना और active sentences को passive में correctly transform करना सिखाता है।

## Core Meaning and Rule

Passive voice **action के receiver** को sentence का subject बनाता है। Verb **be + past participle** के form का use करता है। Doer (agent) को **by** के साथ mention किया जा सकता है या छोड़ा जा सकता है।

> **Rule block:** Object को subject में transform करें। Tense को verb **be** में रखें। **By + agent** तभी use करें जब doer important हो।

| Active | Passive |
|---|---|
| Subject + verb + object | Object + be + past participle (+ by subject) |

## Forms, Patterns, and Sentence Position

### 1. Simple Present

> Active: They **make** cars here.  
> Passive: Cars **are made** here.

**Am/is/are + past participle** use करें।

### 2. Simple Past

> Active: They **built** the bridge.  
> Passive: The bridge **was built**.

**Was/were + past participle** use करें।

### 3. Simple Future

> Active: They **will announce** the results.  
> Passive: The results **will be announced**.

**Will be + past participle** use करें।

### 4. Present Continuous

> Active: They **are building** a new school.  
> Passive: A new school **is being built**.

**Am/is/are + being + past participle** use करें।

### 5. Past Continuous

> Active: They **were painting** the house.  
> Passive: The house **was being painted**.

**Was/were + being + past participle** use करें।

### 6. Present Perfect

> Active: They **have completed** the task.  
> Passive: The task **has been completed**.

**Has/have + been + past participle** use करें।

### 7. Past Perfect

> Active: They **had finished** the work.  
> Passive: The work **had been finished**.

**Had + been + past participle** use करें।

### 8. Future Perfect

> Active: They **will have completed** the project.  
> Passive: The project **will have been completed**.

**Will have + been + past participle** use करें।

### 9. Modals

> Active: You **must complete** this.  
> Passive: This **must be completed**.

**Modal + be + past participle** use करें।

### 10. Modal Perfect

> Active: They **should have informed** us.  
> Passive: We **should have been informed**.

**Modal + have been + past participle** use करें।

## Complete Tense Reference Table

| Tense | Active | Passive |
|---|---|---|
| Simple present | make / makes | am/is/are + made |
| Simple past | made | was/were + made |
| Simple future | will make | will be + made |
| Present continuous | am/is/are making | am/is/are + being + made |
| Past continuous | was/were making | was/were + being + made |
| Present perfect | has/have made | has/have + been + made |
| Past perfect | had made | had + been + made |
| Future perfect | will have made | will have + been + made |
| Modal | must/can/should make | must/can/should + be + made |
| Modal perfect | must have made | must + have been + made |

## Active-to-Passive Transformation Checklist

1. **Active sentence का object identify करें।** यह passive subject बनता है।
2. **Active verb का tense identify करें।** Same tense **be** के साथ use करें।
3. **Main verb का past participle** use करें।
4. **By + agent add करें** सिर्फ अगर doer important हो या unknown हो।
5. **New sentence check करें।** क्या same tense और meaning रखता है?

### Sample 1

> They built the bridge.

- Object: the bridge → new subject.
- Tense: simple past → was/were.
- Past participle: built.
- Passive: The bridge **was built** (by them).

### Sample 2

> He has completed the task.

- Object: the task → new subject.
- Tense: present perfect → has/have been.
- Past participle: completed.
- Passive: The task **has been completed** (by him).

### Sample 3

> They will announce results.

- Object: results → new subject.
- Tense: simple future → will be.
- Past participle: announced.
- Passive: Results **will be announced** (by them).

## Examples That Show the Difference

1. Cars **are made** in Japan. *(simple present)*
2. The bridge **was built** in 1990. *(simple past)*
3. The results **will be announced** tomorrow. *(simple future)*
4. A new hospital **is being constructed**. *(present continuous)*
5. The road **was being repaired** yesterday. *(past continuous)*
6. The task **has been completed**. *(present perfect)*
7. The work **had been finished** before I arrived. *(past perfect)*
8. The project **will have been completed** by next year. *(future perfect)*
9. This **must be done** immediately. *(modal)*
10. We **should have been informed** earlier. *(modal perfect)*

## Exam Traps and Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Wrong Be Form

> Wrong: The work **is doing**.  
> Right: The work **is being done**.

Present continuous passive के लिए **being + past participle** चाहिए।

### Trap 2: Perfect Passive में Been भूलना

> Wrong: The task **has completed**.  
> Right: The task **has been completed**.

Present perfect passive के लिए **has/have + been + past participle** चाहिए।

### Trap 3: Past Participle की जगह Base Form

> Wrong: The bridge was **build**.  
> Right: The bridge was **built**.

हमेशा **past participle** use करें, base form नहीं।

### Trap 4: Intransitive Verbs के साथ Passive

> Wrong: The bed was **slept** by him.  
> Right: He **slept** in the bed.

Intransitive verbs (sleep, come, go, happen) passive नहीं बन सकते क्योंकि उनका object नहीं होता।

### Trap 5: Unnecessary By-Agent

> Wrong: The letter was written **by someone**.  
> Right: The letter was written.

अगर agent unknown या unimportant है, छोड़ दें।

---

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| The work **is doing**. | The work **is being done**. | Present continuous passive. |
| The task **has completed**. | The task **has been completed**. | Need *been* in perfect passive. |
| The bridge was **build**. | The bridge was **built**. | Past participle required. |
| The results **will announced**. | The results **will be announced**. | Need *be* in future passive. |

## Summary

> **Rule:** Passive = receiver as subject + be (right tense में) + past participle। *By + agent* तभी add करें जब ज़रूरत हो।

Memory trick: **Active = किसने किया। Passive = क्या हुआ।**

ये revise करें:

- Cars **are made** in Japan. *(present)*  
- The bridge **was built** in 1990. *(past)*  
- The task **has been completed**. *(present perfect)*  
- The results **will be announced**. *(future)*
