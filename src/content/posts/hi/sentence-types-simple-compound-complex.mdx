## परिचय: Sentence Types क्यों ज़रूरी हैं

ये तीन वाक्य देखिए:

> I studied. *(एक clause, एक idea)*  
> I studied, and I passed. *(दो बराबर ideas)*  
> Because I studied, I passed. *(एक main idea, एक supporting idea)*

तीनों similar reality communicate करते हैं, लेकिन उनकी **structure** अलग है। English sentences को clauses के combination के आधार पर चार types में classify करती है। इन types को समझना error spotting, sentence transformation और exams में well-structured answers लिखने के लिए ज़रूरी है।

---

## मूल अर्थ और नियम

> **Rule box:** **Simple** = एक independent clause। **Compound** = दो या ज़्यादा independent clauses। **Complex** = एक independent + एक या ज़्यादा dependent clauses। **Compound-complex** = दो या ज़्यादा independent clauses + एक या ज़्यादा dependent clauses।

| Sentence Type | Structure | उदाहरण |
|---|---|---|
| Simple | 1 independent clause | I studied. |
| Compound | 2+ independent clauses | I studied, and I passed. |
| Complex | 1 independent + 1+ dependent | Because I studied, I passed. |
| Compound-Complex | 2+ independent + 1+ dependent | Because I studied, I passed, and I celebrated. |

---

## रूप, पैटर्न और वाक्य में स्थिति

### 1. Simple Sentences

Simple sentence में **एक independent clause** होता है — एक subject और एक predicate जो complete thought express करते हैं। इसमें compound subject या compound verb हो सकता है, लेकिन फिर भी केवल एक clause होगा।

> She **studied**. *(एक subject, एक verb)*  
> Rahul and Priya **studied and passed**. *(compound subject, compound verb — फिर भी एक clause)*  
> The old man **sat quietly by the window**. *(modifiers के साथ एक clause)*

Simple sentence छोटा या लंबा हो सकता है। इसे "simple" बनाता है कि इसमें केवल एक clause है, इसकी लंबाई नहीं।

### 2. Compound Sentences

Compound sentence **दो या ज़्यादा independent clauses** को जोड़ता है:
- **Coordinating conjunction** (FANBOYS: for, and, nor, but, or, yet, so) comma के साथ
- **Semicolon** (;)
- **Semicolon + conjunctive adverb** (however, therefore, moreover, आदि)

> I studied hard**,** **and** I passed. *(comma + coordinating conjunction)*  
> I studied hard**;** I passed. *(semicolon)*  
> I studied hard**; therefore,** I passed. *(semicolon + conjunctive adverb)*

**आम ग़लती — comma splice:**

> ❌ I studied hard, I passed.  
> ✅ I studied hard, **and** I passed. / I studied hard**;** I passed.

दो independent clauses को अकेले comma से नहीं जोड़ सकते।

### 3. Complex Sentences

Complex sentence में **एक independent clause** और **एक या ज़्यादा dependent (subordinate) clauses** होते हैं। Dependent clause subordinating conjunction (*because, although, if, when, while, unless, since, after, before*) या relative pronoun (*who, which, that, whose*) से शुरू होता है।

> **Because I studied hard,** I passed. *(dependent + independent)*  
> I passed **because I studied hard.** *(independent + dependent)*  
> **Although she was tired,** she continued working.  
> The man **who lives next door** is a doctor.

**Key point:** Dependent clause अकेले नहीं खड़ा हो सकता। अगर अकेले है, तो वह **fragment** है।

> ❌ **Because I was tired.** I went home.  
> ✅ **Because I was tired,** I went home.

### 4. Compound-Complex Sentences

Compound-complex sentence दोनों की features combine करता है: **दो या ज़्यादा independent clauses** और **कम से कम एक dependent clause**।

> **Because I studied hard,** I passed**,** **and** I celebrated.  
> She was tired**,** **but** she continued working **because the deadline was near**.  
> **When the rain stopped,** we went outside**,** **and** we played.

ये sentences academic और professional writing में आम हैं। इससे आप multiple related ideas को nuance के साथ express कर सकते हैं।

## Decision Checklist

Sentence का type identify करने के लिए:

1. **Independent clauses गिनिए।** क्या हर clause अकेले वाक्य बन सकता है?
2. **Dependent clauses गिनिए।** क्या कोई clause subordinating conjunction या relative pronoun से शुरू होता है और अकेले नहीं खड़ा हो सकता?
3. **Rule लगाइए:**
   - 1 independent, 0 dependent → **Simple**
   - 2+ independent, 0 dependent → **Compound**
   - 1 independent, 1+ dependent → **Complex**
   - 2+ independent, 1+ dependent → **Compound-Complex**

---

## अंतर दिखाने वाले उदाहरण

1. She writes every day. *(simple — एक independent clause)*
2. She writes every day**,** **and** she edits at night. *(compound — दो independent clauses)*
3. **Because she writes every day,** her skills improve. *(complex — dependent + independent)*
4. **Because she writes every day,** her skills improve**,** **and** her confidence grows. *(compound-complex)*
5. The dog barked loudly. *(simple)*
6. The dog barked**,** **and** the cat ran away. *(compound)*
7. **When the dog barked,** the cat ran away. *(complex)*
8. **When the dog barked,** the cat ran away**,** **and** the bird flew off. *(compound-complex)*
9. I studied hard. *(simple)*
10. I studied hard**,** **so** I passed. *(compound)*

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Comma Splice

> ❌ I studied, I passed.  
> ✅ I studied, **and** I passed. / I studied**;** I passed.

दो independent clauses को अकेले comma से नहीं जोड़ सकते। Conjunction या semicolon उपयोग करें।

### Trap 2: Sentence Fragment

> ❌ **Because I was tired.**  
> ✅ **Because I was tired,** I went home.

Dependent clause को independent clause से जोड़ना ज़रूरी है।

### Trap 3: Dependent Clauses को पहचानना

> I know **that he is honest**. — *"that he is honest"* एक dependent clause (noun clause) है। यह **complex** sentence है।

कई छात्र noun clauses गिनना भूलते हैं। कोई भी clause जो अकेले नहीं खड़ा हो सकता, वह dependent है।

### Trap 4: Relative Clauses

> The book **that I borrowed** was excellent. — *"that I borrowed"* एक dependent clause है। यह **complex** sentence है।

Relative clauses (*who, which, that, whose, where, when*) complex sentences बनाते हैं।

## आम गलतियाँ

| ग़लत | सही | क्यों |
|---|---|---|
| I studied, I passed. | I studied, **and** I passed. | Comma splice। |
| Because I was tired. | **Because I was tired,** I went home. | Fragment — main clause चाहिए। |
| The man who is standing there is my brother. *(simple classify)* | यह **complex** है — इसमें relative clause है। | Relative clauses dependent हैं। |

## Quick Practice

Sentence type identify करिए या error ढूढ़िए।

1. She sings and dances. *(simple / compound / complex)*
2. She sings, and he dances. *(simple / compound / complex)*
3. When she sings, everyone listens. *(simple / compound / complex)*
4. Error spotting: I was hungry, I ate quickly.
5. Error spotting: Because the train was late.
6. Error spotting: She left early, she missed the bus.
7. Fill in the blank: I studied hard ___ I passed. (and / because / when)
8. Identify: The girl who won the prize is my sister.
9. Identify: When the bell rang, the students left, and the teacher packed up.
10. Rewrite correctly: Although it was raining. We went out.

### Answer Key

1. **Simple** — compound verb, एक clause।  
2. **Compound** — दो independent clauses।  
3. **Complex** — dependent + independent।  
4. **I was hungry; I ate quickly.** — comma splice।  
5. **Because the train was late, we were delayed.** — fragment ठीक किया।  
6. **She left early, so she missed the bus.** — comma splice।  
7. **and** — compound sentence।  
8. **Complex** — relative clause *who won the prize*।  
9. **Compound-complex** — dependent clause + दो independent clauses।  
10. **Although it was raining, we went out.** — fragment ठीक किया।

## सारांश

> **Rule:** Simple = 1 independent clause। Compound = 2+ independent। Complex = 1 independent + 1+ dependent। Compound-complex = 2+ independent + 1+ dependent।

Memory trick: **"Simple अकेले खड़ा होता है। Compound बराबरों को जोड़ता है। Complex dependence जोड़ता है। Compound-complex दोनों करता है।"**

ये revise करें:

- I studied. *(simple)*  
- I studied, and I passed. *(compound)*  
- **Because I studied,** I passed. *(complex)*  
- **Because I studied,** I passed, and I celebrated. *(compound-complex)*
