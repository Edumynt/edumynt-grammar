## परिचय: "I Am Knowing Him" क्यों गलत है

English learners अक्सर continuous tense को हर present action के लिए use कर देते हैं। लेकिन कुछ verbs action नहीं, **state** बताते हैं। ऐसे verbs को **stative verbs** कहते हैं।

> ❌ I am **knowing** him.  
> ✅ I **know** him.

> ❌ She is **having** a car.  
> ✅ She **has** a car.

Stative verbs usually continuous tense में नहीं आते, क्योंकि वे ongoing action नहीं बताते; वे condition, thought, feeling, possession या perception बताते हैं।

---

## मूल अर्थ और नियम

> **Rule box:** Stative verbs **states** बताते हैं — thoughts, feelings, possession, senses, existence. ये usually **simple tenses** लेते हैं, continuous नहीं। कुछ verbs stative और active दोनों हो सकते हैं, meaning के आधार पर।

Simple idea:

> ✅ I know the answer. *(state of knowledge)*  
> ❌ I am knowing the answer.

---

## Stative Verbs की मुख्य categories

### 1. Thoughts और opinions

ये verbs mind की state बताते हैं।

> I **know** the answer. *(not "am knowing")*  
> She **believes** in hard work.  
> He **understands** the problem.  
> They **remember** the incident.  
> I **think** you are right. *(opinion)*  
> She **doubts** his intentions.

जब *think* opinion बताता है, simple tense use होता है:

> ✅ I think this is correct.  
> ❌ I am thinking this is correct.

---

### 2. Feelings और emotions

Feelings generally states हैं, इसलिए simple tense common है।

> I **love** this song.  
> She **hates** waiting.  
> He **prefers** tea to coffee.  
> They **want** to leave.  
> I **need** your help.  
> She **fears** the worst.

Informal speech में *I am loving this* जैसे forms सुनाई देते हैं, लेकिन standard grammar में:

> ✅ I love this.  
> ⚠️ I am loving this. *(informal/emphatic)*

---

### 3. Possession

Ownership या possession बताने वाले verbs continuous में नहीं आते।

> She **has** a car. *(not "is having")*  
> He **owns** a house.  
> They **possess** great wealth.  
> The book **belongs** to me.  
> This box **contains** important documents.

Common Indian-English error:

> ❌ I am having two brothers.  
> ✅ I have two brothers.

---

### 4. Senses और perception

जब sense verbs किसी quality या perception को state की तरह बताते हैं, simple tense use करें।

> This soup **tastes** delicious.  
> The flower **smells** wonderful.  
> She **looks** tired. *(appears)*  
> It **feels** cold outside.  
> I **hear** a noise.

इनमें adjective आता है, adverb नहीं:

> ❌ This tastes well.  
> ✅ This tastes good.

---

### 5. Existence और measurement states

कुछ verbs existence, cost, weight, appearance जैसी states बताते हैं।

> The problem **exists**.  
> The building **stands** on a hill.  
> This **costs** fifty rupees.  
> The box **weighs** two kilos.  
> It **seems** correct.  
> She **appears** nervous.

> ❌ This is costing fifty rupees. *(general price के लिए wrong)*  
> ✅ This costs fifty rupees.

---

## Verbs जो Stative और Active दोनों हो सकते हैं

कुछ verbs meaning बदलने पर continuous में आ सकते हैं। यही advanced part है।

| Verb | Stative: simple tense | Active: continuous tense |
|---|---|---|
| **think** | I **think** you are right. *(opinion)* | I **am thinking** about the problem. *(mental process)* |
| **have** | She **has** a car. *(possession)* | She **is having** lunch. *(eating)* |
| **see** | I **see** the mountain. *(perceive)* | I **am seeing** the doctor. *(meeting)* |
| **taste** | The soup **tastes** good. *(quality)* | She **is tasting** the soup. *(action)* |
| **smell** | It **smells** wonderful. *(quality)* | He **is smelling** the flower. *(action)* |
| **look** | She **looks** tired. *(appears)* | She **is looking** at the painting. *(action)* |
| **feel** | It **feels** soft. *(quality)* | I **am feeling** the fabric. *(touching)* |
| **weigh** | It **weighs** 5 kg. *(state)* | She **is weighing** the package. *(action)* |
| **be** | He **is** rude. *(character)* | He **is being** rude. *(temporary behaviour)* |

Important contrast:

> He **is** rude. *(उसका nature/character)*  
> He **is being** rude today. *(अभी temporary behaviour)*

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| I am knowing him. | I know him. | Know stative है। |
| She is having a car. | She has a car. | Have possession = stative। |
| This tastes well. | This tastes good. | Taste linking verb है; adjective चाहिए। |
| I am thinking you are right. | I think you are right. | Think opinion = stative। |
| It is seeming correct. | It seems correct. | Seem stative है। |

---

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### Trap 1: *have* को हमेशा stative समझना

> ✅ She has a car. *(possession — stative)*  
> ✅ She is having lunch. *(eating — action)*

Meaning decide करेगा tense।

### Trap 2: *think* के दो meanings

> ✅ I think he is honest. *(opinion)*  
> ✅ I am thinking about the answer. *(process)*

### Trap 3: linking verbs के बाद adverb लगाना

> ❌ The soup tastes well.  
> ✅ The soup tastes good.

*Taste, smell, look, feel, seem* जैसे verbs adjective complement लेते हैं।

## Quick Practice

Correct form चुनें।

1. I ___ the answer. *(am knowing/know)*
2. She ___ a car. *(is having/has)*
3. This soup ___ delicious. *(is tasting/tastes)*
4. Error spotting: I am knowing him well.
5. Error spotting: She is having two brothers.
6. Error spotting: This tastes well.
7. Fill in: He ___ about the problem. *(is thinking/thinks)*
8. Fill in: She ___ tired. *(is looking/looks)*
9. Rewrite correctly: I am having a headache.
10. Choose: He ___ rude today. *(is/is being)*

### Answer Key

1. **know** — stative।
2. **has** — possession stative है।
3. **tastes** — quality/state।
4. **I know him well.**
5. **She has two brothers.**
6. **This tastes good.**
7. **is thinking** — mental process।
8. **looks** — appears/state।
9. **I have a headache.**
10. **is being** — temporary behaviour।

## Summary

> **Rule:** Stative verbs जैसे *know, have, love, believe, taste, seem* states बताते हैं और usually simple tense लेते हैं। लेकिन कुछ verbs meaning बदलने पर active बनकर continuous tense ले सकते हैं।

Memory trick: **States don't act: know, have, love, believe — simple tense. Think, have, taste, see — meaning check करो.**
