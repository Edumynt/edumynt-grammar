## Introduction: Highlight करने के लिए Sentence को Split करना

एक cleft sentence एक idea को दो parts में split करता है ताकि एक element पर emphasis हो।

> I met Rahul yesterday. → **It was Rahul that** I met yesterday.  
> I need more time. → **What I need is** more time.

Information वही है। Focus shift होता है। यह article दो main types cover करता है: **it-clefts** और **wh-clefts**।

---

## Core Meaning and Rule

> **Rule box:** Emphasis के लिए clefts use करें। **It + be + focus + that/who clause.** **Wh-clause + be + focus.**

| Type | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| It-cleft | It + be + focus + that/who + clause | It was **Rahul that** I met. |
| Wh-cleft | What + subject + verb + be + focus | What I **need is** more time. |

---

## Forms, Patterns, and Sentence Position

### 1. It-Cleft: Object Emphasize करना

> I met **Rahul** yesterday. → It was **Rahul that** I met yesterday.  
> She needs **help**. → It is **help that** she needs.

### 2. It-Cleft: Subject Emphasize करना

> **Rahul** helped me. → It was **Rahul who** helped me.  
> **The noise** woke me up. → It was **the noise that** woke me up.

### 3. It-Cleft: Time Emphasize करना

> I met him **yesterday**. → It was **yesterday that** I met him.

Formal it-clefts में time के लिए **that** use करें (*when* नहीं)।

### 4. Wh-Cleft (Pseudo-Cleft)

> I need **more time**. → What I need **is** more time.  
> She wants **honesty**. → What she wants **is** honesty.  
> He did **leave early**. → What he did **was** leave early.

Wh-clefts में *be* के बाद bare infinitive आता है (*to + verb* नहीं)।

### 5. All-Cleft

> I want **to leave**. → All I want **is to leave**.

---

## Decision Checklist

1. **क्या emphasize करना है?** Subject, object, time, या action?
2. **Subject/object/time focus के लिए:** It-cleft: *It + be + focus + that/who + clause*।
3. **Action या general focus के लिए:** Wh-cleft: *What + subject + verb + be + focus*।
4. **People के लिए who, things और time के लिए that** use करें।
5. **Wh-clefts में be के बाद bare infinitive** use करें।

### Sample 1

> It was yesterday when I met Rahul.

Formal it-cleft में **that** use करें।

> It was yesterday **that** I met Rahul.

### Sample 2

> What he did was he left early.

Wh-cleft में bare infinitive।

> What he did was **leave** early.

## Exam Traps and Usage Notes

### Trap 1: It-Clefts में When

> Wrong: It was yesterday **when** I met him.  
> Right: It was yesterday **that** I met him.

Formal it-clefts में **that** use करें।

### Trap 2: Wh-Clefts में To-Infinitive

> Wrong: What he did was **to leave** early.  
> Right: What he did was **leave** early.

Wh-clefts में bare infinitive use करें।

### Trap 3: Who vs That

> Wrong: It was Rahul **that** helped me. *(acceptable but less precise)*  
> Right: It was Rahul **who** helped me.

Formal writing में people के लिए **who** use करें।

### Trap 4: Clefts का Overuse

Clefts emphasis के लिए हैं। Default sentence structure के रूप में use न करें।

---

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| It was yesterday **when** I met him. | It was yesterday **that** I met him. | Use *that* in it-clefts. |
| What he did was **to leave** early. | What he did was **leave** early. | Bare infinitive in wh-clefts. |

## Quick Practice

Cleft sentences में rewrite करें।

1. I need rest. (wh-cleft)
2. Rahul broke the window. (it-cleft, Rahul emphasize)
3. She realised the truth last night. (it-cleft, time emphasize)
4. Error spotting: It was last week when I visited them.
5. Error spotting: What she did was to apologise.
6. Fill in the blank: It was the teacher ___ explained this. (who/that)
7. Rewrite: I want peace. (wh-cleft)
8. Choose: What he needs ___ more practice. (is / are)

### Answer Key

1. **What I need is** rest.  
2. **It was Rahul who** broke the window.  
3. **It was last night that** she realised the truth.  
4. **It was last week that** I visited them.  
5. **What she did was apologise.**  
6. **who** — people के लिए।  
7. **What I want is** peace.  
8. **is** — singular.

## Summary

> **Rule:** It-cleft = It + be + focus + that/who + clause. Wh-cleft = What + clause + be + focus (bare infinitive).

Memory trick: **It-cleft star की ओर इशारा करता है। Wh-cleft point को wrap करता है।**

ये revise करें:

- It was **Rahul that** I met.  
- What I need **is** more time.  
- It was **yesterday that** I realised.  
- What he did was **leave** early.
