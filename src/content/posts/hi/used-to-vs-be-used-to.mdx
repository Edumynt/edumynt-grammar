## परिचय: Same Words, लेकिन दो अलग Structures

"Used to" और "be used to" almost same दिखते हैं, इसलिए learners इन्हें mix कर देते हैं:

> गलत: I am used to **wake** early.  
> सही: I am used to **waking** early.

लेकिन यह compare करें:

> सही: I **used to wake** early.

दोनों sentences में "used to" है, लेकिन grammar अलग है। **Used to + base verb** past habit या past state बताता है जो अब true नहीं है। **Be used to + noun/gerund** familiarity बताता है: कोई चीज़ experience की वजह से normal लगती है।

यह difference writing, speaking, error spotting और fill-in-the-blank questions में important है क्योंकि "to" के बाद आने वाला word पूरी तरह बदल जाता है।

---

## Core Meaning और Rule

- **Used to + base verb** = past habit या past state.
- **Be used to + noun / -ing form** = किसी चीज़ से familiar होना.
- **Get used to + noun / -ing form** = familiar बनना.

> **Rule box:** Past habit वाले **used to** के बाद base verb use करें। **Be/get used to** के बाद noun या -ing form use करें।

Compare करें:

> I **used to live** in Jaipur.  
> I **am used to living** in Jaipur.

पहले sentence का मतलब है: मैं past में Jaipur में रहता था, पर शायद अब नहीं।  
दूसरे sentence का मतलब है: Jaipur में रहना मेरे लिए normal है।

## Forms, Patterns और Sentence Position

### Past Habit के लिए Used To

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| subject + used to + base verb | I **used to play** football. |
| subject + did not use to + base verb | She **did not use to like** tea. |
| did + subject + use to + base verb | **Did** you **use to live** here? |

Questions और negatives में "did" के साथ usually **use to** आता है, **used to** नहीं।

> सही: Did you **use to** play cricket?  
> Speech में common लेकिन exams में avoid करें: Did you **used to** play cricket?

### Familiarity के लिए Be Used To

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| subject + am/is/are used to + noun | She **is used to** the noise. |
| subject + am/is/are used to + -ing | I **am used to waking** early. |
| subject + was/were used to + noun/-ing | They **were used to working** late. |

### Familiar बनने के लिए Get Used To

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| subject + get/gets/got used to + noun | You will **get used to** the climate. |
| subject + get/gets/got used to + -ing | He **got used to driving** on the left. |

## Decision Checklist

इन questions को order में पूछें:

1. Sentence का meaning है "past में regularly होता था, अब नहीं"? **Used to + base verb**.
2. Meaning है "यह चीज़ familiar या normal है"? **Be used to + noun/-ing**.
3. Meaning है "familiar बनना"? **Get used to + noun/-ing**.
4. "Used" से पहले be का कोई form है? फिर "to" के बाद base verb मत लगाएँ।
5. पहले "did" है? Careful grammar में **use to** use करें, **used to** नहीं।

### Checklist Examples

> I ___ sleep late during college.

यह past habit है। Correct: I **used to sleep** late during college.

> I am used to ___ late.

यहाँ "am used to" है, इसलिए next word -ing form होना चाहिए। Correct: I am used to **sleeping** late.

> Did you ___ live near the market?

यहाँ "did" है, इसलिए base form use करें। Correct: Did you **use to** live near the market?

## Examples That Show the Difference

1. I **used to wake** early.  
   Past में मैं regularly early उठता था। शायद अब नहीं।

2. I **am used to waking** early.  
   Early उठना मेरे लिए normal है।

3. She **used to work** in a bank.  
   Past situation; शायद अब वह वहाँ काम नहीं करती।

4. She **is used to working** under pressure.  
   Pressure में काम करना उसके लिए familiar है।

5. We **used to live** in a small town.  
   Past state.

6. We **are used to** city traffic now.  
   Noun phrase के साथ familiarity.

7. Did you **use to play** cricket?  
   "Did" के साथ correct question form.

8. He **got used to speaking** in public.  
   वह gradually comfortable हो गया।

9. They **were used to cold weather**.  
   Cold weather उनके लिए strange नहीं था।

10. The new employee **will get used to** the system soon.  
    Familiarity time के साथ develop होगी।

## सबसे बड़ा Difference: To के बाद Verb कैसा होगा?

"To" tricky है क्योंकि इसके दो roles हो सकते हैं।

**Used to + base verb** में "to" infinitive-like structure का हिस्सा है:

> used to **wake**  
> used to **play**  
> used to **live**

**Be used to** में "to" preposition है। Preposition के बाद noun या gerund आता है:

> used to **noise**  
> used to **waking**  
> used to **working**

इसलिए यह sentence wrong है:

> गलत: I am used to **wake** early.

"Am used to" के बाद noun-like form चाहिए:

> सही: I am used to **waking** early.

## Used To vs Would

**Used to** और **would** दोनों repeated past actions describe कर सकते हैं।

> When we were children, we **used to play** outside.  
> When we were children, we **would play** outside.

लेकिन **used to** actions और states दोनों के साथ काम करता है:

> I **used to have** long hair.  
> She **used to know** everyone in the village.

"Would" usually past states के साथ natural नहीं है:

> Awkward: I **would have** long hair.  
> बेहतर: I **used to have** long hair.

इसलिए अगर sentence past state describe कर रहा है, **used to** safer है।

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### 1. Be Used To Past Habit नहीं है

> गलत: I am used to wake early.  
> सही: I am used to **waking** early.

"Am" structure बदल देता है। Sentence familiarity के बारे में है, past habit के बारे में नहीं।

### 2. Did + Use To

Standard exam grammar में "did" के बाद base form use करें:

> सही: Did you **use to** study here?  
> सही: I did not **use to** drink coffee.

Casual speech में कुछ लोग "did you used to" बोलते हैं, लेकिन कई tests इसे wrong मानते हैं।

### 3. Get Used To Change दिखाता है

> I **am used to** the heat.  
> I **am getting used to** the heat.

पहले का meaning है heat already normal लगती है। दूसरे का meaning है process अभी चल रहा है।

### 4. Present Habits के लिए Used To का Present Form नहीं है

Present habits के लिए "use to" मत लगाएँ।

> गलत: I use to go to the gym every day.  
> सही: I go to the gym every day.  
> सही: I usually go to the gym every day.

## Common Mistakes

| गलत | सही | क्यों |
|---|---|---|
| I am used to **wake** early. | I am used to **waking** early. | Be used to + gerund/noun. |
| I **used to waking** early. | I **used to wake** early. | Past habit में base verb. |
| Did you **used to** play cricket? | Did you **use to** play cricket? | Did के बाद base form. |
| She is **use to** the noise. | She is **used to** the noise. | Be used to में used चाहिए। |
| I use to study at night. | I study at night. / I usually study at night. | Present habit के लिए "use to" नहीं। |
| He got used to **drive** in traffic. | He got used to **driving** in traffic. | Get used to + gerund/noun. |

## Quick Practice

1. I ___ live near the railway station.  
   a) used to  b) am used to

   a) travel  b) travelling

3. Did they ___ play together?  
   a) use to  b) used to

4. You will get used to ___ online classes.  
   a) attending  b) attend

5. I am used to wake up at 5 a.m.

6. He used to working in Delhi.

7. Did she used to speak French?

Rewrite या fill in the blank.

8. ऐसा sentence लिखें जिसका meaning हो: "Past में मैं regularly chess खेलता था।"

9. Fill in: She is used to ___ spicy food. (eat)

10. Fill in: We did not ___ live in this area.

### Answer Key

1. **used to** — past state.  
2. **travelling** — be used to + gerund.  
3. **use to** — did + base form.  
4. **attending** — get used to + gerund.  
5. I am used to **waking** up at 5 a.m.  
6. He **used to work** in Delhi.  
7. Did she **use to** speak French?  
8. I **used to play** chess.  
9. **eating** — She is used to eating spicy food.  
10. **use to** — did not use to live.

## Summary

> **Final rule:** **Used to + base verb** = past habit या past state. **Be/get used to + noun या -ing** = familiarity या familiar बनना।

Memory trick: अगर **used** से पहले **be** है, तो **to** के बाद base verb मत लगाएँ।

ये examples revise करें:

> I **used to wake** early.  
> I **am used to waking** early.  
> She **is used to** the noise.  
> Did you **use to** play cricket?
