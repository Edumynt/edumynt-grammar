## Introduction: Pronoun Errors बहुत Common Sentences में छिपते हैं

Pronoun mistakes अक्सर बच जाते हैं क्योंकि sentence conversation में familiar लगता है।

> Wrong: **Me and Rahul** went to the market.  
> Right: **Rahul and I** went to the market.

Meaning clear है, लेकिन grammar नहीं। Pronoun subject का part है, इसलिए subject form **I** चाहिए, object form **me** नहीं।

Pronoun error spotting सिर्फ **he**, **she**, **they**, और **it** जैसे words पहचानने का test नहीं है। यह case, agreement, reference और reflexive pronouns test करता है। अगर pronoun अपने noun से match नहीं करता, या reader को clear नहीं है कि वह किसको refer कर रहा है, sentence weak या wrong हो जाता है।

Related pronoun usage के लिए [Who vs Whom](/hi/posts/who-vs-whom/) और [Subject-Verb Agreement with Either Or](/hi/posts/subject-verb-agreement-either-or/) revise करें।

## Core Meaning and Rule

Pronoun किसी noun को replace करता है या उसकी ओर point करता है। जिस noun को pronoun refer करता है, उसे antecedent कहते हैं।

> **Rule box:** Pronoun को अपने noun से number, gender और person में match करना चाहिए, और उसका reference clear होना चाहिए।

Pronoun errors usually चार areas से आते हैं:

1. **Case** — subject form या object form?  
   - **I** went; between you and **me**
2. **Agreement** — singular या plural?  
   - each student and **his or her/their** book
3. **Reference** — pronoun exactly किसको refer कर रहा है?  
   - Ravi told Aman that **he** was late. कौन late था?
4. **Reflexive misuse** — myself, yourself, himself, etc.  
   - **I am** Keshav, not **Myself Keshav**.

## Forms, Patterns, and Sentence Position

### 1. Subject Pronouns and Object Pronouns

| Function | Pronouns | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Subject | I, we, he, she, they, who | **She** called me. |
| Object | me, us, him, her, them, whom | Riya called **me**. |
| Possessive determiner | my, our, his, her, their | This is **my** bag. |
| Possessive pronoun | mine, ours, his, hers, theirs | This bag is **mine**. |

Quick test: दूसरे noun को हटाकर देखें।

> Rahul and **I** went. → **I** went.  
> The teacher praised Rahul and **me**. → The teacher praised **me**.

### 2. Pronouns After Prepositions

Preposition के बाद object form use करें।

| Preposition Phrase | Correct Form |
|---|---|
| between you and ___ | between you and **me** |
| for Rahul and ___ | for Rahul and **me** |
| with Riya and ___ | with Riya and **her** |
| to my brother and ___ | to my brother and **me** |

इसीलिए standard English में **between you and I** wrong है।

### 3. Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement

Pronoun अपने noun से agree करना चाहिए।

| Antecedent | Pronoun Pattern |
|---|---|
| The boy | he / his / him |
| The girl | she / her |
| The students | they / their / them |
| Each student | his or her / their, depending style |
| The company | it / its |

Modern English में gender unknown या irrelevant हो तो singular **they** common है।

> Every student must bring **their** book.  
> Every student must bring **his or her** book.

दोनों expected style के हिसाब से acceptable हो सकते हैं। Traditional exams **his or her** या rewritten plural sentence prefer कर सकते हैं: **All students must bring their books.**

### 4. Reflexive Pronouns

Reflexive pronouns **-self** या **-selves** पर end होते हैं: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves.

जब subject और object same person या thing हों, तब इन्हें use करें।

> I hurt **myself**.  
> She prepared **herself** for the test.

सिर्फ polite या formal लगने के लिए reflexive pronoun use न करें।

> Wrong: **Myself Keshav**.  
> Right: **I am Keshav**.

## Decision Checklist for Pronoun Error Spotting

यह method use करें:

1. **Pronoun find करें।**  
2. **उसका noun/antecedent find करें।** वह किसको refer कर रहा है?  
3. **Case check करें।** Pronoun subject, object, possessive या preposition के बाद है?  
4. **Agreement check करें।** Singular/plural, gender और person.  
5. **Clarity check करें।** Reader clearly समझ सकता है कि pronoun किसके लिए है?  
6. **Reflexive use check करें।** क्या **myself/himself/themselves** सच में needed है?

### Sample 1

> Me and Rahul went to the market.

Pronoun subject का part है। Subject form **I** use करें, और standard polite order में दूसरे person को पहले रखें।

> **Rahul and I** went to the market.

### Sample 2

> This is between you and I.

**Between** preposition है। Preposition के बाद object form आता है।

> This is between **you and me**.

### Sample 3

> Myself Keshav.

यहाँ **Myself** subject introduction के रूप में use नहीं हो सकता।

> **I am Keshav**.

## Examples That Show the Difference

1. **Rahul and I** went to the library.  
2. The teacher called **Rahul and me**.  
3. This secret is between **you and me**.  
4. **I am Keshav**, not **Myself Keshav**.  
5. Every student must bring **his or her book**.  
6. Modern usage में every student must bring **their book** भी common है।  
7. All students must bring **their books**.  
8. Riya and **she** are cousins.  
9. The prize was given to Riya and **her**.  
10. The dog wagged **its** tail.

Main idea यह है कि impressive लगने वाला pronoun न चुनें; grammatical role के हिसाब से pronoun चुनें।

## Exam Traps and Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Compound Subjects with I/Me

Wrong: Me and my friend attended the seminar.  
Right: My friend and I attended the seminar.

दूसरे noun को हटाकर test करें:

> Me attended the seminar. ❌  
> I attended the seminar. ✅

### Trap 2: Compound Objects with I/Me

Wrong: The principal congratulated Rahul and I.  
Right: The principal congratulated Rahul and me.

फिर वही test:

> The principal congratulated I. ❌  
> The principal congratulated me. ✅

### Trap 3: Pronouns After Prepositions

Wrong: There is no problem between you and I.  
Right: There is no problem between you and me.

**Between**, **for**, **with**, **to**, **from**, और **of** जैसे prepositions usually object pronouns लेते हैं।

### Trap 4: Everyone, Each, Either, Neither

**Everyone**, **each**, **either**, और **neither** formal grammar में अक्सर singular माने जाते हैं।

> Each of the boys has brought **his** book.  
> Each student must bring **his or her** book.  
> Each student must bring **their** book. (modern English में common)

Exams में expected style carefully पढ़ें। Safe rewrite अक्सर plural होता है:

> All students must bring **their books**.

### Trap 5: Unclear Reference

Unclear: Ravi told Aman that **he** was wrong.  
Clear: Ravi told Aman, “**You** are wrong.”  
Clear: Ravi admitted that **he** was wrong.

Pronoun reader को guess करने पर मजबूर नहीं करना चाहिए।

### Trap 6: Reflexive Pronouns Used for Formality

Wrong: Please give the file to **myself**.  
Right: Please give the file to **me**.

Wrong: **Myself** will attend the meeting.  
Right: **I** will attend the meeting.

Reflexive pronouns तभी use करें जब उनका real grammatical job हो।

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Correct | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Me and Rahul** went. | **Rahul and I** went. | Subject position में **I** चाहिए। |
| This is between **you and I**. | This is between **you and me**. | Preposition के बाद object form आता है। |
| **Myself Keshav**. | **I am Keshav**. | Reflexive pronoun subject introduce नहीं करता। |
| The teacher praised Rahul and **I**. | The teacher praised Rahul and **me**. | Object position में **me** चाहिए। |
| Each student must bring **their books**. | Each student must bring **his or her book** / **their book**. | Formal style में singular antecedent singular reference लेता है। |
| The company changed **their** policy. | The company changed **its** policy. | Company को इस sentence में singular माना गया है। |
| Riya and **her** are friends. | Riya and **she** are friends. | Subject position में subject form चाहिए। |

## Quick Practice

### A. Choose the Correct Option

1. Rahul and ___ went to the meeting.  
   a) me  b) I  c) myself  d) mine

2. The teacher spoke to Riya and ___.  
   a) I  b) me  c) myself  d) mine

3. This is between you and ___.  
   a) I  b) me  c) myself  d) mine

4. Every student must submit ___ assignment.  
   a) their  b) his or her  c) his/her  d) all may be acceptable by style

### B. Spot the Error

5. Myself will call you tomorrow.  
6. The dog hurt itself while jumping.  
7. Me and Ankit are preparing for the test.

### C. Rewrite Correctly

8. The manager invited Riya and I.  
9. Ravi told Aman that he had failed. (Reference clear बनाइए.)  
10. Everyone should bring their books. (Formally singular बनाइए.)

### Answer Key

1. **I** — subject position.  
2. **me** — **to** का object.  
3. **me** — preposition **between** के बाद object form.  
4. **all may be acceptable by style** — traditional exams **his or her** prefer कर सकते हैं; modern usage singular **their** accept करता है।  
5. **Myself → I** — wrong reflexive use.  
6. No error — **itself** correctly **dog** को refer करता है।  
7. **Me and Ankit → Ankit and I** — subject position.  
8. The manager invited **Riya and me**.  
9. Example: **Ravi told Aman, “You have failed.”** / **Ravi admitted that he had failed.**  
10. Everyone should bring **his or her book**.

## Summary

Pronoun error spotting आसान हो जाती है जब आप पूछते हैं कि pronoun sentence में कौन-सा काम कर रहा है।

> **Final rule box:**  
> Subject role → I, he, she, they, who.  
> Object role/preposition → me, him, her, them, whom.  
> Pronoun अपने noun से match करे और clearly refer करे।  
> Reflexive pronouns को real reason चाहिए।

Memory trick: **पहले noun find करें, फिर pronoun का job find करें।**

ये correct forms revise करें:

> **Rahul and I** went.  
> This is between **you and me**.  
> **I am Keshav**.  
> Every student must bring **his or her/their book**, depending on style.
