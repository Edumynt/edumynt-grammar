## परिचय: "They Elected Him President"

कुछ verbs सिर्फ direct object नहीं लेते; वे object के बाद एक और word/phrase लेते हैं जो object को describe या rename करता है। इसे **object complement** कहते हैं।

> They elected him **president**. *(president = "him" को rename करता है)*  
> The news made me **sad**. *(sad = "me" को describe करता है)*  
> We consider him **honest**. *(honest = object complement)*

Object complement sentence की hidden लेकिन important structure है। Exam grammar में यह खासकर adjective/adverb और unnecessary **as** errors में दिखती है।

---

## मूल अर्थ और नियम

> **Rule box:** **Object complement** direct object के बाद आता है और direct object को describe या rename करता है। Common verbs: *make, call, find, consider, elect, name, appoint, declare, keep, paint, dye*.

Pattern:

> Subject + Verb + Object + Object Complement

Example:

> The news made **me** **sad**.

यहाँ **me** object है और **sad** object complement है, क्योंकि sad "me" की condition बताता है।

---

## Forms और Patterns

### 1. Verb + Object + Noun

Object complement noun भी हो सकता है। यह object को rename करता है।

> They elected him **president**.  
> She named the baby **Arya**.  
> They appointed her **manager**.  
> The committee declared him **the winner**.  
> We crowned her **queen**.

इन examples में complement object की new identity या role बताता है।

---

### 2. Verb + Object + Adjective

Object complement adjective भी हो सकता है। यह object की quality/state बताता है।

> The news made me **sad**.  
> She painted the wall **blue**.  
> We found the task **difficult**.  
> He kept the room **clean**.  
> I consider him **honest**.

ध्यान दें: adjective object को describe करता है, action को नहीं। इसलिए adverb नहीं आएगा।

> ❌ The news made me sadly.  
> ✅ The news made me sad.

---

### 3. Common Verbs That Take Object Complements

| Verb | Example |
|---|---|
| **make** | It made me happy. |
| **call** | They called him a hero. |
| **find** | I found it interesting. |
| **consider** | We consider her talented. |
| **elect** | They elected her president. |
| **name** | They named the ship Titanic. |
| **appoint** | He was appointed captain. |
| **declare** | The judge declared him innocent. |
| **keep** | Keep the room clean. |
| **paint** | She painted the door red. |
| **dye** | She dyed her hair black. |

## Object Complement vs Direct Object

Direct object verb का receiver होता है। Object complement direct object के बारे में extra information देता है।

> They elected **him** **president**.

- **him** = direct object
- **president** = object complement

> She painted **the wall** **blue**.

- **the wall** = direct object
- **blue** = object complement

अगर complement हटाएँ तो sentence grammatical हो सकता है, लेकिन meaning incomplete या different हो सकता है:

> They elected him. *(किस position पर? unclear)*  
> They elected him president. *(complete meaning)*

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| They elected him as president. | They elected him **president**. | Elect के साथ usually "as" नहीं। |
| The news made me sadly. | The news made me **sad**. | Object complement adjective है। |
| We consider him as honest. | We consider him **honest**. | Consider के साथ "as" unnecessary। |
| She painted the door redly. | She painted the door **red**. | Door को describe करना है। |

---

## Exam Traps और Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Adjective को adverb बना देना

> ❌ I found the book interestingly.  
> ✅ I found the book interesting.

Here **interesting** book को describe करता है, finding action को नहीं।

### Trap 2: Unnecessary "as"

कुछ verbs के बाद English में सीधे complement आता है:

> ✅ They elected her captain.  
> ❌ They elected her as captain.

> ✅ We consider him honest.  
> ❌ We consider him as honest.

Note: कुछ contexts में *regard as* correct है:

> We regard him **as** honest.

लेकिन **consider** और **elect** के साथ exam grammar में usually direct complement prefer होता है।

### Trap 3: Passive form में complement पहचानना

Passive में भी complement रह सकता है:

> They appointed her manager.  
> She was appointed manager.

> The judge declared him innocent.  
> He was declared innocent.

3. **honest** — no "as"।

## Summary

> **Rule:** Object complements direct object को describe या rename करते हैं। Object complement adjective या noun हो सकता है। Adjective use करें, adverb नहीं। *Elect, consider, name, appoint* जैसे verbs के साथ unnecessary **as** से बचें।

Memory trick: **Object complements describe the object. Adjective, not adverb. No 'as' with elect and consider.**
