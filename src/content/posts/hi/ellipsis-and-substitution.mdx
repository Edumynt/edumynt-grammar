## Introduction: कम बोलना, वही मतलच

Good English unnecessary repetition से बचती है। **Ellipsis** context से understood words को omit करता है। **Substitution** उन्हें छोटे words जैसे *one*, *do*, *so*, *neither* से replace करता है।

> I can swim and she can swim too. → I can swim and she **can too**. *(ellipsis)*  
> I need a pen. Do you have **one**? *(substitution)*  
> He said he would call, and he **did**. *(substitution)*

दोनों grammatically correct और natural हैं। यह article आपको इन्हें use करना सिखाता है।

---

## Core Meaning and Rule

> **Rule box:** तभी omit या substitute करें जब missing meaning clear और grammatically recoverable हो।

| Technique | Word used | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Ellipsis | (nothing — words omitted) | She can sing and he can **too**. |
| Substitution with "one" | one/ones | I need a pen. Do you have **one**? |
| Substitution with "do" | do/does/did | He said he would, and he **did**. |
| Substitution with "so" | so | I think **so**. / I hope **so**. |
| Substitution with "neither/nor" | neither/nor | I don't like it. **Neither** do I. |

---

## Forms, Patterns, and Sentence Position

### 1. Ellipsis After Auxiliaries

> She can sing and he can **too**. *(sing omitted)*  
> I will come and she will **too**.  
> If you are ready, I am **too**.

Auxiliary (*can*, *will*, *am*) रहता है; main verb omit होता है।

### 2. Substitution with "One"

> I need a **pen**. Do you have **one**?  
> I like the red **shirt**. Do you have **one**?  
> These **shoes** are better than those **ones**.

**One** singular countable noun replace करता है। **Ones** plural replace करता है।

### 3. Substitution with "Do"

> He said he would call, and he **did**.  
> She promised to come, and she **did**.  
> I told him to leave, and he **did**.

**Did** main verb phrase को replace करता है।

### 4. Substitution with "So"

> Will it rain? I think **so**.  
> Is she coming? I hope **so**.  
> Do you like it? I believe **so**.

**So** that-clause को replace करता है thinking, hoping, believing verbs के बाद।

### 5. Substitution with "Neither/Nor"

> I don't like coffee. **Neither** do I.  
> She can't swim, **nor** can he.  
> I haven't finished, **neither** has she.

**Neither/nor** + inversion = "also not"।

## Decision Checklist

1. **क्या repetition है?** → Ellipsis या substitution consider करें।
2. **क्या meaning repeated words के बिना clear है?** → Ellipsis ठीक है।
3. **क्या noun repeat हो रहा है?** → *One/ones* use करें।
4. **क्या verb phrase repeat हो रहा है?** → *Do/does/did* use करें।
5. **क्या that-clause है?** → *So* use करें।
6. **क्या negative agreement है?** → *Neither/nor* + inversion use करें।

## Examples That Show the Difference

1. She can dance and he can **too**. *(ellipsis)*
2. I need a pen. Do you have **one**? *(substitution)*
3. He said he would come, and he **did**. *(substitution)*
4. Will it rain? I think **so**. *(substitution)*
5. I don't like tea. **Neither** do I. *(substitution)*
6. If you are ready, I am **too**. *(ellipsis)*
7. I like these shoes better than those **ones**. *(substitution)*
8. She promised to help, and she **did**. *(substitution)*
9. Is he coming? I hope **so**. *(substitution)*
10. I can't swim, **nor** can my brother. *(substitution)*

## Exam Traps and Usage Notes

### Trap 1: Ellipsis जब Meaning Unclear हो

> Wrong: She likes him and I **too**. *(ambiguous)*  
> Right: She likes him and I **do too**. *(clear)*

### Trap 2: One for Uncountable Nouns

> Wrong: I need water. Do you have **one**?  
> Right: I need water. Do you have **any**?

**One** सिर्फ countable nouns के लिए।

### Trap 3: Neither Without Inversion

> Wrong: I don't like it. Neither **I do**.  
> Right: I don't like it. Neither **do I**.

**Neither** के बाद inversion ज़रूरी (auxiliary subject से पहले)।

---

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Right | Why |
|---|---|---|
| I need water. Do you have **one**? | Do you have **any**? | *One* for countable only. |
| Neither **I do**. | Neither **do I**. | Inversion after *neither*. |

## Quick Practice

Ellipsis या substitution use करें।

1. She can sing and I can ___.
2. I need a notebook. Do you have ___?
3. He said he would help and he ___.
4. Is she coming? I think ___.
5. Error spotting: I don't like it. Neither I do.
6. Error spotting: I need milk. Do you have one?
7. Fill in the blank: She promised to come, and she ___. (do)
8. Rewrite: I like this dress better than that dress.
9. Choose: Will they win? I hope ___. (so / one)
10. Fill: I can't dance, ___ can my sister. (neither / nor)

### Answer Key

1. **too** *(ellipsis)*  
2. **one** *(substitution)*  
3. **did** *(substitution)*  
4. **so** *(substitution)*  
5. **Neither do I.**  
6. **Do you have any?**  
7. **did**  
8. **I like this dress better than that one.**  
9. **so**  
10. **neither** — inversion required.

## Summary

> **Rule:** जब meaning clear हो तो omit या substitute करें। Nouns के लिए *one*, verbs के लिए *do*, clauses के लिए *so*, negative agreement के लिए *neither/nor*।

Memory trick: **अगर recover कर सकते हैं, तो remove कर सकते हैं।**

ये revise करें:

- She can sing and he can **too**.  
- Do you have **one**?  
- He said he would, and he **did**.  
- I don't like it. **Neither** do I.
